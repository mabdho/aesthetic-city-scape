15 Best Things to Do in Bandung, Indonesia (2025 Guide)

Nestled amidst volcanic mountains and lush tea plantations, Bandung is Indonesia's cool and creative heart. Fondly nicknamed 'Paris van Java' for its colonial-era European ambiance and vibrant fashion scene, this West Java capital offers a refreshing escape from the tropical heat. Its unique appeal lies in the seamless blend of natural wonders, from ethereal crater lakes to steaming volcanoes, with a bustling urban life filled with art-deco architecture, trendy cafes, and endless shopping opportunities. Whether you're an adventure seeker, a culture enthusiast, or a foodie eager to sample Sundanese cuisine, you'll find an incredible array of activities. This guide will walk you through the very best things to do in Bandung, ensuring a memorable and multifaceted journey into one of Indonesia's most beloved cities.

Top 5 Must-Visit Attractions

  • Kawah Putih (The White Crater)
  • Tangkuban Perahu Volcano
  • Saung Angklung Udjo
  • Jalan Braga
  • Gedung Sate

Complete Guide to Bandung Attractions

Kawah Putih (The White Crater)

Kawah Putih is a truly otherworldly sight. This stunning volcanic crater lake, located about 50 kilometers south of Bandung, mesmerizes visitors with its surreal, milky turquoise water that changes color with the weather and sulfur concentration. The 'White Crater' gets its name from the surrounding stark white soil, bleached by acidic gases, creating a dramatic and photogenic landscape against the dark, skeletal trees that fringe its shores. The high acidity of the lake means there is no life within it, adding to its mysterious and desolate beauty. The cool mountain air and the mystical mist that often shrouds the crater make a visit here feel like stepping onto another planet. It's an absolute must-see for nature lovers and photographers.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Ciwidey, South Bandung. The best way to reach it is by renting a private car with a driver or joining a tour. From the main gate, you can take a shared minivan (Ontang-Anting) to the crater rim.

Opening Hours: Daily, 7:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Cost: Approx. IDR 75,000 for international tourists, IDR 27,000 for domestic tourists. Additional fees for vehicles and the Ontang-Anting shuttle.

Website: https://www.kawahputih.id/

Tangkuban Perahu Volcano

One of West Java's most famous volcanoes, Tangkuban Perahu offers the unique experience of driving right up to the edge of its main crater, Ratu Crater. The name, which means 'upturned boat' in Sundanese, comes from a local legend about its shape. From the viewpoint, you can gaze into the vast, steaming caldera, witness bubbling mud and sulfurous gases rising from its depths, and appreciate the raw power of nature. Besides the main crater, visitors can hike to the smaller Domas Crater. Here, you can get much closer to the volcanic activity, boiling eggs in the hot springs or enjoying a therapeutic mud foot spa. The surrounding area is filled with stalls selling souvenirs and local snacks.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Lembang, about 30 km north of Bandung. Accessible by car, taxi, or as part of a Lembang tour.

Opening Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Cost: Approx. IDR 300,000 on weekends/holidays and IDR 200,000 on weekdays for international tourists. Lower prices for domestic visitors.

Website: https://twatangkubanparahu.com/

Saung Angklung Udjo

Experience the heart and soul of Sundanese culture at Saung Angklung Udjo. This vibrant cultural workshop and performance venue is dedicated to preserving and promoting the angklung, a traditional Indonesian musical instrument made from bamboo tubes. The daily performances are a joyous spectacle of music and dance, presented by a troupe of talented local children. The show is interactive and incredibly engaging. You'll not only listen to beautiful angklung orchestras but also get the chance to learn how to play one yourself, guided by the cheerful performers. The experience culminates in a massive, harmonious orchestra of visitors playing together. It's a heartwarming and unforgettable cultural immersion.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Jalan Padasuka in East Bandung. Easily reachable by taxi or ride-hailing services.

Opening Hours: Performances typically run daily in the afternoon, around 3:30 PM. Check website for exact times.

Cost: Approx. IDR 120,000 for international adults, with discounts for students and children.

Website: https://www.angklungudjo.com/

Jalan Braga

Take a nostalgic walk down Jalan Braga (Braga Street), the historical heart of Bandung. In the colonial era, this was the city's most fashionable street, lined with chic boutiques, cafes, and restaurants, earning Bandung its 'Paris van Java' moniker. Today, Jalan Braga retains much of its old-world charm, with beautifully preserved art-deco buildings now housing art galleries, cozy coffee shops, and antique stores. By day, it's a great spot for photography and appreciating the architecture. By night, the street comes alive with a laid-back vibe, as locals and tourists alike flock to its restaurants and bars. It's the perfect place to soak up Bandung's history, enjoy a cup of local coffee, and watch the world go by.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Centrally located in Bandung city. Easily accessible on foot from nearby hotels, or by taxi.

Opening Hours: 24/7 access, but shops and cafes have their own operating hours, typically 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM.

Cost: Free to walk around. Costs vary for cafes and shops.

Website: N/A (Public street)

Gedung Sate

Gedung Sate is Bandung's most iconic architectural landmark and the seat of the West Java provincial government. Built in the 1920s, its design is a masterpiece of Dutch colonial architecture with a unique Indonesian twist. The building is famous for the skewer-like ornament on its central pinnacle, which resembles a 'sate' (satay), hence its popular name. While it is an active government office, visitors can admire the grand facade from the outside and explore the well-manicured gardens. There is also a museum inside, Museum Gedung Sate, which details the history of its construction and the development of Bandung. The building is particularly beautiful when illuminated at night.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in central Bandung, near Lapangan Gasibu. Easily reached by taxi or public transport.

Opening Hours: Grounds are accessible during the day. Museum hours are typically Tuesday-Sunday, 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM.

Cost: Free to view from the outside. A small entrance fee applies for the museum (approx. IDR 5,000).

Website: https://gedungsate.jabarprov.go.id/

Farmhouse Lembang

A charming and wildly popular attraction, Farmhouse Lembang transports you to a quaint European countryside village. With its Dutch-style farmhouses, beautiful flower gardens, and even a flock of fluffy sheep you can feed, it's a paradise for families and Instagram enthusiasts. Visitors can rent traditional European costumes for the perfect photo op. The complex also features a petting zoo, souvenir shops, and various food stalls. A unique feature is that your entrance ticket can be exchanged for a cup of fresh milk or a sausage, adding to the farm experience. Don't miss the 'Hobbit House,' a whimsical replica that's a favorite photo spot.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on the main road in Lembang, north of Bandung. It's on the route to other Lembang attractions and best reached by car or taxi.

Opening Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM (may vary on weekends).

Cost: Approx. IDR 30,000 per person.

Website: Check official social media channels for the latest information.

Floating Market Lembang

Unlike the traditional floating markets of Thailand, Floating Market Lembang is a modern recreational park centered around a scenic lake where food is sold from colorful boats. Visitors don't float; they walk along the lakeside platforms and purchase a wide variety of delicious Sundanese, Indonesian, and Western food from the vendors on the water. Beyond the unique culinary experience, the park offers a host of other activities. There's a mini city for kids, a water park, boat rentals, and a beautiful Japanese-themed garden. It’s a complete family-friendly destination where you can eat, play, and relax in a beautiful natural setting.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Lembang, north of Bandung. It's a popular stop on a Lembang day trip.

Opening Hours: Weekdays 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM, Weekends 9:00 AM - 8:00 PM.

Cost: Entrance fee is approx. IDR 35,000, which includes a welcome drink. Transactions inside use a token system.

Website: https://www.floatingmarketlembang.com/

Dusun Bambu Family Leisure Park

Dusun Bambu, or 'Bamboo Village,' is an eco-tourism park that beautifully integrates recreation with nature. Set against the backdrop of Mount Burangrang, the park is designed around bamboo architecture and sustainable principles. It offers a serene and picturesque environment for relaxation and family activities. Key attractions include the iconic Lutung Kasarung, a series of bird-cage-like dining pods suspended in the trees, and the Purbasari restaurant set by a tranquil lake. You can also enjoy paddle boating, cycling, or simply strolling through its lush gardens and rice paddies. It's an ideal spot to escape the city's hustle and enjoy a peaceful day out.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at the foot of Mount Burangrang in Cisarua, Lembang. Best accessed by private car or taxi.

Opening Hours: Daily, 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM.

Cost: Entrance fee is around IDR 40,000. Dining and activities are charged separately.

Website: https://dusunbambu.id/

Cihampelas Walk (Ciwalk)

Cihampelas Walk, affectionately known as Ciwalk, is an open-air shopping mall that offers a refreshing alternative to typical enclosed shopping centers. With its lush greenery, outdoor spaces, and a relaxed atmosphere, it's a favorite hangout spot for locals and tourists alike. The mall features a wide range of international and local fashion brands, a cinema, and numerous cafes and restaurants. What makes it unique is its blend of modern retail with natural elements. At night, the twinkling lights create a magical ambiance. Above the street level, the new Teras Cihampelas (Cihampelas Terrace) skywalk provides a dedicated space for street vendors and pedestrians, separating them from the traffic below.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Jalan Cihampelas, famous for its factory outlets. It's a central and easily accessible location.

Opening Hours: Daily, 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM.

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: https://cihampelaswalk.com/

Trans Studio Bandung

For thrill-seekers and families looking for a dose of adrenaline-pumping fun, Trans Studio Bandung is the answer. As one of the world's largest indoor theme parks, it offers a weather-proof day of entertainment with over 20 rides and a variety of spectacular shows. The park is divided into three themed zones: Studio Central (Hollywood-themed), Lost City (adventure-themed), and Magic Corner. Highlights include the Yamaha Racing Coaster, which launches riders backward at high speed, a giant swing, and numerous simulators and 4D experiences. The park is conveniently located within the Trans Studio Mall complex, which also includes a massive shopping mall and luxury hotels, making it a one-stop entertainment hub.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at Trans Studio Bandung Supermall on Jalan Gatot Subroto. Easily reachable by taxi.

Opening Hours: Daily, 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM (hours may vary).

Cost: Approx. IDR 200,000 - 300,000, depending on the day (weekday vs. weekend/holiday).

Website: https://www.transentertainment.com/transstudio/bandung

NuArt Sculpture Park

NuArt Sculpture Park is a haven for art lovers, showcasing the incredible works of Indonesian sculptor Nyoman Nuarta, the artist behind the monumental Garuda Wisnu Kencana statue in Bali. The park spans several hectares and features a gallery and an outdoor sculpture garden where dozens of his large-scale copper and brass creations are displayed amidst lush greenery. The sculptures are powerful and dynamic, often depicting themes of nature, humanity, and Indonesian mythology. The gallery offers a look into the artist's creative process, with smaller pieces, maquettes, and sketches. It's a serene and thought-provoking space that provides a deep dive into the world of one of Indonesia's most celebrated contemporary artists.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the northern part of Bandung. Best reached by taxi or ride-hailing service.

Opening Hours: Wednesday - Monday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Closed on Tuesdays).

Cost: Approx. IDR 50,000 per person.

Website: https://nuartsculpturepark.com/

Curug Dago (Dago Waterfall) & Dago Pakar

Nestled in the Dago Pakar conservation area, Curug Dago is a modest waterfall with significant historical value. Inscriptions left by Thai kings Chulalongkorn (Rama V) and Prajadhipok (Rama VII) from the 19th and early 20th centuries are carved into the rocks nearby. While the waterfall itself may be less spectacular than others, the surrounding forest park, known as Taman Hutan Raya Ir. H. Djuanda, is a major draw. This vast forest reserve is a green lung for Bandung, offering cool air and scenic walking trails. A popular activity is to explore the 'Gua Jepang' and 'Gua Belanda' (Japanese and Dutch Caves), dark tunnels built during World War II and the colonial period. The area is perfect for a light trek and a breath of fresh air close to the city.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Dago Atas (Upper Dago), North Bandung. Accessible by car or motorcycle.

Opening Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM.

Cost: Entrance fee for the forest park is approx. IDR 15,000.

Website: https://tahuradjuanda.jabarprov.go.id/

Patenggang Lake (Situ Patenggang)

Another gem in the Ciwidey region, Situ Patenggang is a beautiful lake surrounded by rolling tea plantations. The atmosphere here is serene and romantic, steeped in a local legend about a pair of lovers. The main attraction is the 'Batu Cinta' (Love Stone) on a small island, which can be reached by renting a colorful boat. A popular spot on the lake's edge is the Glamping Lakeside Rancabali, famous for its massive boat-shaped restaurant, Pinisi Resto, which offers stunning panoramic views of the lake and tea fields. It's a wonderful place to relax, enjoy the cool air, and sip a cup of local tea while taking in the magnificent scenery.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Ciwidey, South Bandung, close to Kawah Putih. It's best to combine a visit to both in one day trip.

Opening Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM.

Cost: Entrance fee is approx. IDR 25,000, with additional costs for boat rentals and access to the Pinisi Resto area.

Website: Check local tourism sites for information.

Geology Museum of Bandung

A fascinating and educational stop, the Geology Museum of Bandung is one of the finest of its kind in Southeast Asia. Housed in a historic art-deco building, the museum holds an extensive collection of geological materials, including rocks, minerals, and an impressive array of fossils. The star attractions are the life-sized replicas of dinosaur skeletons, including a massive Tyrannosaurus Rex, and the fossilized remains of the 'Java Man' (Homo erectus). The exhibits are well-organized, covering the geology of Indonesia, natural resources, and the history of life on Earth. It's an excellent destination for families and anyone with an interest in natural history.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Centrally located on Jalan Diponegoro, right next to Gedung Sate.

Opening Hours: Monday - Thursday 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM, Saturday - Sunday 9:00 AM - 2:00 PM (Closed on Fridays and public holidays).

Cost: Very affordable, approx. IDR 10,000.

Website: https://museum.geologi.esdm.go.id/

Kopi Luwak Cikole

For a uniquely Indonesian experience, visit Kopi Luwak Cikole in the Lembang area. This agritourism spot offers an educational tour about 'Kopi Luwak,' one of the world's most expensive and controversial coffees. The coffee beans are famously processed after being eaten and excreted by the Asian palm civet (luwak). Here, you can learn about the entire process, see the civets (often in cages, which raises ethical questions for some visitors), and witness the roasting of the beans. The tour culminates in a tasting session where you can sample the famous brew, which is known for its smooth, less bitter profile. It's a quirky and interesting stop for coffee aficionados who are curious about this legendary beverage.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Cikole, Lembang, on the way to or from Tangkuban Perahu.

Opening Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM.

Cost: Free entry to the site, but you pay for the coffee tasting, which is around IDR 100,000 per cup.

Website: N/A (Agritourism destination)

Planning Your Visit to Bandung

Getting Around

Getting around Bandung is best done using ride-hailing apps like Gojek and Grab, which offer affordable car and motorcycle taxi services. For tourist areas like Lembang or Ciwidey, which are outside the city center, renting a car with a private driver for the day is highly recommended for convenience and efficiency. Traditional taxis are also available. For short distances in the city, the local 'angkot' (public minivans) are a cheap but often crowded option.

Where to Stay

For first-timers, the Dago area is excellent, offering cool air, great cafes, and proximity to shopping. The Cihampelas and Setiabudi areas are perfect for shoppers, with numerous factory outlets. For a historical vibe, stay near Jalan Braga to be close to colonial architecture and chic cafes. Recommended hotels include The Papandayan (Luxury), Padma Hotel Bandung (Luxury with stunning views), and Moxy Bandung (Mid-range/Stylish).

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Bandung is during the dry season, from June to September, when you'll have sunny days perfect for exploring the outdoor attractions. However, Bandung's high altitude keeps it relatively cool year-round. Be aware that Bandung is a hugely popular weekend getaway for Jakartans, so traffic and crowds swell significantly from Friday evening to Sunday. For a more relaxed experience, plan your visit on weekdays.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: South Bandung Adventure - Travel to Ciwidey to visit the ethereal Kawah Putih in the morning, followed by the scenic Patenggang Lake and Glamping Lakeside's boat-shaped restaurant. En route, stop at a strawberry farm. Day 2: North Bandung & Lembang - Head north to the Tangkuban Perahu volcano. Afterward, explore the themed attractions of Lembang, such as Farmhouse Lembang or the Floating Market. Day 3: City Culture & Shopping - Explore Bandung's urban heart. Start at Gedung Sate and the Geology Museum, walk down historic Jalan Braga, enjoy an afternoon cultural show at Saung Angklung Udjo, and finish with shopping at Cihampelas Walk or a factory outlet.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bandung expensive?
Bandung is generally considered very affordable for travelers. Street food and local restaurants are incredibly cheap. Transportation via ride-hailing apps is also very budget-friendly. The main costs come from accommodation, with options ranging from budget guesthouses to luxury hotels, and entrance fees to major tourist attractions, which often have higher prices for international tourists. Overall, it's a great value-for-money destination.
How many days do you need in Bandung?
A trip of 3 to 4 days is ideal to experience Bandung properly. This allows for one full day to explore the southern attractions (like Kawah Putih), one full day for the northern Lembang area (like Tangkuban Perahu), and one to two days to explore the city itself, including shopping, cafes, and cultural sites like Saung Angklung Udjo.
Is Bandung safe?
Yes, Bandung is generally a safe city for tourists. The locals are friendly and welcoming. However, like in any large city, you should take standard precautions. Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas to avoid petty theft, and be cautious of the hectic traffic when crossing streets. Using reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps is recommended for transport.
What is Bandung famous for?
Bandung is famous for several things: its cool climate and stunning volcanic landscapes, earning it the nickname 'The Flower City'; its rich colonial Dutch art-deco architecture, leading to the name 'Paris van Java'; its vibrant factory outlet shopping scene; its thriving creative community and trendy coffee shops; and its delicious Sundanese cuisine, known for fresh vegetables, sambal, and grilled fish.
