15 Best Things to Do in Suva, Fiji (2025 Guide)

Welcome to Suva, the bustling heart and political capital of Fiji. A world away from the tranquil island resorts, Suva offers a vibrant slice of authentic Fijian urban life. This cosmopolitan port city is a melting pot of cultures, reflected in its diverse cuisine, colonial-era architecture, and lively markets. Home to the University of the South Pacific, it has a youthful, energetic vibe that pulses through its streets. While it may not have white sand beaches, its lush rainforest parks, fascinating historical sites, and rich cultural institutions provide a different, equally compelling Fijian experience. From exploring archaeological treasures to hiking to hidden waterfalls, there are countless adventures to be had. This guide will walk you through all the very best things to do in Suva for an unforgettable trip.

Complete Guide to Suva Attractions

Fiji Museum

Nestled within Thurston Gardens, the Fiji Museum is an essential first stop for anyone looking to understand the nation's rich and complex history. It houses a remarkable collection of archaeological material dating back 3,700 years, alongside cultural objects reflecting Fiji's indigenous and colonial past. The exhibits cover everything from cannibalism and tribal warfare to maritime history and the influence of the Indo-Fijian community. Highlights include the massive Ratu Finau, a 13-meter long traditional double-hulled canoe (drua), and the rudder from the HMS Bounty, made famous by the mutiny. It provides invaluable context for the rest of your Fijian journey.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Thurston Gardens, a short walk or taxi ride from central Suva.

Opening Hours: Monday - Saturday: 9:30 AM - 4:30 PM; Sunday: Closed.

Cost: Approximately FJD $10 for adults.

Website: www.fijimuseum.org.fj

Suva Municipal Market

Immerse yourself in the vibrant chaos of local life at the Suva Municipal Market, one of the largest and most colourful open-air markets in the South Pacific. This two-story building is a sensory explosion, with vendors selling a dazzling array of fresh tropical fruits, vegetables, seafood, and fragrant spices. Upstairs, you'll find a section dedicated to kava (yaqona), where you can see the roots being pounded and sold. It's a fantastic place for people-watching, photography, and trying local produce. Be sure to engage with the friendly vendors, who are often happy to explain their goods and offer a taste.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Harris Road, near the main bus station in central Suva.

Opening Hours: Monday - Saturday: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM. Busiest on Saturdays.

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: N/A

Colo-I-Suva Forest Park

Escape the city's humidity with a trip to Colo-I-Suva Forest Park, a lush oasis of tropical rainforest just a 15-minute drive from downtown. This 2.5-sq-km nature reserve features walking trails that wind through mahogany plantations and native jungle, leading to crystal-clear natural pools and picturesque waterfalls. The park is a haven for birdwatchers, with chances to spot species like the Fiji Goshawk and the Sulphur-breasted Musk Parrot. The highlight for many is taking a refreshing dip in one of the swimming holes, especially the popular rope-swing spot. It's the perfect half-day trip for nature lovers.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the Colo-i-Suva bus from the main bus station or hire a taxi.

Opening Hours: Daily: 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM.

Cost: Entrance fee of approximately FJD $5 per person.

Website: N/A

Thurston Gardens

Originally founded as the Suva Botanical Gardens in 1881, Thurston Gardens provides a peaceful green space in the heart of the city. The gardens boast a wide collection of Fijian and other tropical flora, including palms, gingers, lilies, and an iconic clock tower at its center. It's a lovely place for a leisurely stroll, a picnic, or simply to relax on a bench under the shade of ancient trees. The gardens are also home to the Fiji Museum, making it easy to combine a visit to both attractions for a perfect afternoon of culture and relaxation.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Cakobau Road, next to the Grand Pacific Hotel and Albert Park.

Opening Hours: Daily: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM.

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: N/A

Government Buildings and Albert Park

A centerpiece of Suva's colonial past, the majestic Government Buildings are an architectural marvel. Completed in 1939, this grand Art Deco complex houses the Fijian Parliament, the offices of the Prime Minister, and the High Court. While access inside is restricted, its impressive facade and clock tower are a must-see. Across the road lies Albert Park, a historic sporting ground where Australian aviator Sir Charles Kingsford Smith famously landed his plane, the Southern Cross, during the first trans-Pacific flight in 1928. It's a popular spot for local rugby games and public events.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Southern Cross Road in the city center, opposite the Grand Pacific Hotel.

Opening Hours: Grounds are publicly accessible.

Cost: Free to view from the outside.

Website: N/A

Sacred Heart Cathedral

A striking landmark on Suva's skyline, the Sacred Heart Cathedral is a testament to the city's rich history and architectural diversity. Built in 1902, this Roman Catholic cathedral features imposing twin towers and grand Romanesque architecture, constructed from sandstone brought over from Sydney. The interior is equally impressive, with beautiful stained-glass windows, soaring ceilings, and a sense of tranquility that offers a peaceful respite from the busy city streets. Visitors are welcome to attend a service or simply step inside to admire its historic beauty.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Pratt Street in the heart of Suva.

Opening Hours: Open daily for visitors and services. Be respectful of worship times.

Cost: Free, donations are welcome.

Website: N/A

Suva Handicraft Centre

For authentic, locally made souvenirs, skip the generic tourist shops and head straight to the Suva Handicraft Centre. This bustling marketplace is filled with stalls where local artisans sell their creations directly to the public. You'll find a wide variety of goods, including intricately carved wooden tanoa bowls, woven baskets, colourful sulus (sarongs), masi (tapa cloth) art, and unique shell jewelry. It's the perfect place to find a meaningful keepsake from your trip to Fiji while directly supporting local craftspeople. Don't be afraid to chat with the vendors and learn about the stories behind their crafts. Gentle bargaining is acceptable but remember to be respectful.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Centrally located on the waterfront, a short walk from the Municipal Market.

Opening Hours: Monday - Saturday: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM.

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: N/A

The Grand Pacific Hotel

Even if you're not staying there, the Grand Pacific Hotel (GPH) is a historical landmark worth visiting. Established in 1914, this iconic 'Grand Old Lady' of Suva has hosted royalty, including Queen Elizabeth II, and dignitaries for over a century. After falling into disrepair, it was magnificently restored and reopened in 2014, recapturing its colonial-era grandeur. Stroll through the lobby, admire the elegant architecture, and enjoy a drink or a meal at one of its waterfront restaurants or bars. It offers a glimpse into a bygone era of glamour and is a cornerstone of Suva's heritage.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Prominently located on Victoria Parade, opposite Albert Park.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7. Restaurants and bars have specific hours.

Cost: Free to enter the lobby and common areas. Prices for food and drink vary.

Website: www.grandpacifichotel.com.fj

Mount Korobaba

For the adventurous traveler, a hike up Mount Korobaba offers a rewarding challenge and spectacular panoramic views. At 422 meters, this is one of the highest peaks near Suva, and the trail to the summit is a steep and often muddy trek through dense forest. The round-trip hike can take 3-4 hours and requires a good level of fitness. Your efforts will be rewarded at the top with breathtaking vistas of Suva Harbour, the Rewa Delta, and the surrounding islands. It's a fantastic way to experience Fiji's rugged interior. Be sure to wear sturdy hiking shoes, bring plenty of water, and ideally, go with a local guide or in a group.

Practical Information

How to Get There: The trailhead is in Lami, a short taxi or bus ride west of Suva.

Opening Hours: Best attempted during daylight hours, ideally in the morning to avoid afternoon heat.

Cost: Free. Hiring a guide is recommended and will have a cost.

Website: N/A

Fiji Untold Show

Experience a world-class display of Fijian culture through contemporary song and dance at the Fiji Untold show. This high-energy production tells the story of ancient Fiji through a captivating performance that combines traditional motifs with modern choreography, lighting, and sound. It's often compared to shows like Cirque du Soleil for its artistry and professionalism. Presented by the talented VOU Dance Company, the show is a vibrant and emotional journey into Fijian mythology and spirit. It's an unforgettable evening of entertainment that provides a deeper appreciation for the artistic traditions of the islands.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Performances are typically held at the VOU Hub in Nadi or at special venues in Suva. Check their schedule for Suva dates.

Opening Hours: Show times vary. Check the official website for the latest schedule.

Cost: Ticket prices vary depending on seating.

Website: www.fijiuntold.com

Suva Point (Nasese Seawall)

Join the locals for a walk, jog, or relaxing evening at Suva Point, also known as the Nasese Seawall. This long, paved promenade stretches along the coastline of the upscale Nasese suburb and is a popular spot for exercise and socializing, especially in the cool hours of the early morning and late afternoon. The seawall offers beautiful views across Laucala Bay and is a prime location to watch the sunset paint the sky. You'll find food stalls selling snacks and drinks, making it a perfect spot to unwind after a day of sightseeing and observe everyday life in Suva.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the Nasese suburb, a short taxi or bus ride east of the city center.

Opening Hours: Accessible 24/7.

Cost: Free.

Website: N/A

TappooCity Suva

For a dose of modern Suva and some retail therapy, visit TappooCity. This six-story shopping mall is the largest and most modern in the city, offering a striking contrast to the traditional markets. Inside, you'll find a range of department stores, international brands, boutiques, and a large food court with both local and international options. The top floor features a cinema, making it a popular entertainment hub for residents. It's a great place to escape the heat or rain, grab a coffee, and pick up anything you might need, from high-end fashion to Fijian-themed souvenirs.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Thomson Street, in the main central business district.

Opening Hours: Monday - Saturday: 9:00 AM - 9:00 PM; Sunday: 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM.

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: www.tappoo.com.fj

Suva City Library (Carnegie Library)

The Suva City Library, also known as the Carnegie Library, is not just a place for books but also a significant historical building. Funded by Scottish-American philanthropist Andrew Carnegie, it was built in 1909 and stands as one of the many Carnegie libraries constructed around the world. Its charming colonial architecture makes it a noteworthy landmark. Step inside to find a quiet and cool sanctuary from the bustling city. While the collection is primarily for local use, visitors are welcome to browse the shelves and appreciate the history of this century-old institution that has long been a pillar of the Suva community.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Victoria Parade, in the central part of the city.

Opening Hours: Monday - Friday: 9:30 AM - 6:00 PM; Saturday: 9:00 AM - 1:00 PM.

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: N/A

Parliament of Fiji

The current Parliament of Fiji building is a fascinating blend of modern design and traditional Fijian motifs. Located on a sprawling complex, its architecture is inspired by a traditional Fijian village layout. It's the center of Fiji's political life and a symbol of its modern identity as a republic. While public tours of the interior may require advance booking and are subject to parliamentary schedules, the impressive exterior and surrounding grounds are worth seeing. It offers insight into the contemporary governance of the nation, providing a modern counterpoint to the city's colonial-era government buildings.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Ratu Sukuna Road, a short taxi ride from the city center.

Opening Hours: Tours are by appointment and subject to sitting schedules. Check official sources.

Cost: Free.

Website: www.parliament.gov.fj

Beqa Lagoon Day Trip (from Suva)

For an unforgettable aquatic adventure, take a day trip from Suva to Beqa Lagoon. Famous worldwide for its shark dives, Beqa (pronounced 'Benga') offers the chance to safely dive cage-free with multiple species of sharks, including bull sharks, tiger sharks, and reef sharks. It's a bucket-list experience for certified scuba divers. Not a diver? Many operators also offer snorkeling trips, village visits, and fire-walking ceremonies on Beqa Island, the traditional home of this incredible ritual. Tour operators typically arrange transport from Suva, making it an accessible, albeit full-day, excursion from the capital.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Tours depart from Pacific Harbour, about a 45-minute drive from Suva. Most operators offer hotel pickups from Suva.

Opening Hours: Full-day tours, typically starting early in the morning.

Cost: Expensive. Shark dives can cost FJD $300-$500, snorkeling/sightseeing trips are less.

Website: Multiple tour operators, such as Beqa Adventure Divers or Aqua-Trek.

Planning Your Visit to Suva

Getting Around

Suva is a walkable city, especially the downtown area. For longer distances, local buses are extremely cheap but can be crowded and don't follow a strict schedule. Taxis are plentiful and affordable; always confirm the fare with the driver before starting your trip or ensure the meter is used. Ride-sharing apps are also becoming more common.

Where to Stay

The central business district (CBD) offers convenience, with hotels like Tanoa Plaza Hotel and Quest Suva putting you within walking distance of most attractions. For a more luxurious and historic experience, the Grand Pacific Hotel on the waterfront is unmatched. The suburb of Nasese offers a quieter, more residential feel with lovely guesthouses and access to the seawall.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Suva is during Fiji's dry season, which runs from April to October. During these months, you can expect less rainfall, lower humidity, and pleasantly warm temperatures, making it ideal for exploring the city on foot and hiking in the nearby parks. The wet season (November to March) is characterized by heavy downpours and high humidity.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: Cultural Core. Start at the Fiji Museum and Thurston Gardens. Walk to the nearby Government Buildings and Albert Park. Explore the colonial architecture of the city center, including the Sacred Heart Cathedral, and finish the day at the bustling Suva Municipal Market. Day 2: Nature & Crafts. Take a morning trip to Colo-I-Suva Forest Park for a hike and a swim. Return to the city for lunch, then browse for authentic souvenirs at the Suva Handicraft Centre. In the evening, take a relaxing walk along the Nasese Seawall (Suva Point) at sunset. Day 3: Adventure or Relaxation. Choose your adventure: an early morning hike up Mount Korobaba for stunning views, or a full-day trip to Beqa Lagoon for world-class shark diving or snorkeling. Alternatively, have a relaxed day of shopping at TappooCity and enjoy a final Fijian meal at a waterfront restaurant.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Suva expensive?
Compared to Fiji's tourist-focused resort areas in Nadi and the Mamanuca Islands, Suva is relatively affordable. Local food from markets and small eateries is very cheap. Taxis and public transport are also inexpensive. Accommodation ranges from budget-friendly guesthouses to the luxurious Grand Pacific Hotel. Overall, a traveler can experience Suva on a more modest budget than other parts of Fiji.
How many days do you need in Suva?
Two to three days are generally sufficient to explore the main highlights of Suva. This gives you enough time to see the key cultural sites like the Fiji Museum, experience the markets, and enjoy a nature excursion to Colo-I-Suva. If you plan on doing a full-day trip like hiking Mount Korobaba or diving in Beqa Lagoon, adding an extra day is recommended.
Is Suva safe?
Suva is generally safe for tourists, but like any major city, it requires common sense and awareness. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag snatching, can occur, particularly in crowded areas like markets and bus stations. It is advisable to avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night. Keep your valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings. Overall, Fijians are incredibly friendly and welcoming people.
What is Suva famous for?
Suva is famous for being the political, economic, and cultural capital of Fiji and a major hub for the entire South Pacific region. It is known for its impressive British colonial architecture, the extensive Fiji Museum, the vibrant Suva Municipal Market, and its status as a multicultural melting pot. Unlike the resort towns, Suva is famous for offering a look into the real, working life of urban Fiji.
