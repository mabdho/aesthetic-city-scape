15 Best Things to Do in Indianapolis, USA (2025 Guide)

Welcome to Indianapolis, the "Crossroads of America" and a city that beautifully blends high-octane thrills with heartfelt Midwestern charm. While world-famous for the Indianapolis 500, the largest single-day sporting event on the globe, Indy offers so much more than just racing. This surprisingly green city boasts sprawling urban parks, a picturesque canal walk, and a thriving cultural scene with world-class museums and vibrant arts districts. From cheering on the Colts at Lucas Oil Stadium to exploring poignant historical monuments, there's a dynamic energy here that captivates visitors. Whether you're a family looking for educational fun, a sports fanatic, or a culture aficionado, you'll find an incredible array of activities. This guide will walk you through all the very best things to do in Indianapolis, ensuring your visit is packed with unforgettable moments in the heart of Hoosier country.

Complete Guide to Indianapolis Attractions

Indianapolis Motor Speedway Museum

No trip to Indianapolis is complete without a pilgrimage to the racing capital of the world. Located within the famed 2.5-mile oval, the Indianapolis Motor Speedway Museum is a shrine to motorsport history. The museum showcases an incredible collection of winning cars, including dozens of Indy 500 champions, alongside trophies, memorabilia, and interactive exhibits that tell the story of this legendary track. For the ultimate experience, take one of the track tours. The 'Kiss the Bricks' tour allows you to ride a bus around the oval and stop at the iconic start/finish line, a tradition for every race winner. It provides a unique perspective on the sheer scale of the speedway and the courage of the drivers who compete here.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 4750 W 16th St, Indianapolis, IN 46222. Located inside the racetrack oval, accessible via Gate 2.

Opening Hours: Daily 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM (hours can vary on event days)

Cost: Adults ~$15, Youth (6-15) ~$8. Track tours are an additional cost.

Website: https://imsmuseum.org/

The Children's Museum of Indianapolis

Consistently ranked as one of the best children's museums in the world, this is a must-visit for families, but truly offers something for all ages. Spanning five floors of interactive exhibits, the museum is an enormous playground for the mind. You can explore a massive dinosaur exhibit, travel to outer space in a simulated space station, discover ancient cultures in National Geographic's 'Treasures of the Earth', and learn about historical figures who changed the world, like Anne Frank and Ruby Bridges. The outdoor Riley Children's Health Sports Legends Experience is a highlight, offering 7.5 acres of sports-themed activities where kids can practice basketball, football, hockey, and even pedal car racing. It’s a place of wonder, discovery, and endless fun.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 3000 N Meridian St, Indianapolis, IN 46208. A short drive or bus ride north of downtown.

Opening Hours: Typically Tuesday-Sunday, 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM. Open daily in summer.

Cost: Prices vary by day (~$20-$35). Book online in advance for better rates.

Website: https://www.childrensmuseum.org/

White River State Park

This 250-acre urban oasis serves as the cultural heart of downtown Indianapolis. More than just a park, it's a hub that connects many of the city's top attractions, including the Indianapolis Zoo, the Eiteljorg Museum, the NCAA Hall of Champions, and the Indiana State Museum. It's the perfect place to start your exploration of the city. You can rent a paddle boat on the Central Canal, catch an outdoor concert at the TCU Amphitheater, walk or bike along scenic trails, or simply relax on the great lawn with stunning skyline views. The park's blend of green space and cultural institutions makes it a unique and essential Indianapolis experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 801 W Washington St, Indianapolis, IN 46204. Located on the western edge of downtown.

Opening Hours: Park is open 5:00 AM - 11:00 PM daily. Attraction hours vary.

Cost: Free to enter the park. Individual attractions have their own admission fees.

Website: https://www.whiteriverstatepark.org/

Central Canal Walk

The Central Canal Walk is a 3-mile loop that offers a serene escape right in the middle of the city. Originally dug in the 1830s, this revitalized waterway is now lined with beautiful landscaping, public art, and memorials. It's a popular spot for locals and tourists alike to walk, jog, or bike. One of the best ways to experience the canal is by renting a pedal boat or kayak, giving you a unique vantage point of the downtown skyline. The path connects directly to White River State Park and passes by several museums and cafes, making it a scenic and practical way to navigate the western side of downtown.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Accessible from multiple points in downtown and White River State Park.

Opening Hours: 24/7

Cost: Free to walk. Boat rentals have a fee.

Website: https://www.visitindy.com/indianapolis-central-canal-walk

Monument Circle

Considered the physical and symbolic heart of Indianapolis, Monument Circle is a must-see. At its center stands the magnificent Soldiers and Sailors Monument, a 284-foot limestone obelisk dedicated to the common Hoosier soldier. It is one of the world's most outstanding monuments and is an iconic symbol of the city. For a small fee, you can climb the 330 steps (or take a tiny elevator) to the observation deck for a breathtaking 360-degree view of the city. The circle itself is a bustling hub of activity, surrounded by shops, the Hilbert Circle Theatre (home to the Indianapolis Symphony Orchestra), and restaurants. Throughout the year, it hosts festivals, concerts, and the spectacular 'Circle of Lights' during the holiday season.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 1 Monument Cir, Indianapolis, IN 46204. The center of downtown Indianapolis.

Opening Hours: Monument Interior/Observation Deck: Friday-Sunday, 11:00 AM - 5:30 PM (hours can vary). The circle is always accessible.

Cost: Free to visit the circle. A small fee (~$2) to access the observation deck.

Website: https://www.iwm.in.gov/ssm-info

Newfields

Newfields is not just an art museum; it's a 152-acre campus that seamlessly integrates art and nature. The main building, the Indianapolis Museum of Art (IMA), features an impressive and diverse collection spanning 5,000 years of art history, from European masters to contemporary works. A highlight is the extensive collection of Neo-Impressionist paintings and the works of J.M.W. Turner. Outside the museum walls, the experience continues. Explore The Garden, with its beautifully manicured landscapes, or wander through the Virginia B. Fairbanks Art & Nature Park, a 100-acre park with large-scale art installations set in woodlands and meadows. In the summer, enjoy outdoor movies, and in winter, be dazzled by the 'Winterlights' holiday display. It's a place where you can spend an entire day immersed in beauty.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 4000 Michigan Rd, Indianapolis, IN 46208. North of downtown, accessible by car or bus.

Opening Hours: Tuesday-Sunday, 11:00 AM - 5:00 PM (extended hours on some evenings).

Cost: Adults ~$20, Youth (6-17) ~$14. Includes access to the entire campus.

Website: https://discovernewfields.org/

Eiteljorg Museum of American Indians and Western Art

Located in White River State Park, the Eiteljorg Museum offers a captivating look into the art, history, and cultures of the American West and Native peoples of North America. It is one of only two such museums east of the Mississippi. The collection is stunning, featuring classic Western art by Frederic Remington and Charles Russell alongside a powerful and growing collection of contemporary Native American art. The museum's architecture itself is a work of art, inspired by the land and cultures it represents. The exhibits are thoughtfully curated, providing deep insights into the diverse stories and experiences that have shaped the American continent. It's a powerful and educational experience that broadens perspectives.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 500 W Washington St, Indianapolis, IN 46204. In White River State Park.

Opening Hours: Monday-Saturday: 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM; Sunday: 12:00 PM - 5:00 PM.

Cost: Adults ~$18, Youth (5-17) ~$10.

Website: https://eiteljorg.org/

NCAA Hall of Champions

For any college sports fan, the NCAA Hall of Champions is a required stop. This interactive museum celebrates the legacy of the student-athlete across all 24 NCAA sports. The first floor is a tribute to the present, with trivia challenges, current team rankings, and a multi-sport simulator where you can try your hand at various sports. The second floor honors the past, delving into the history of collegiate athletics and celebrating the individuals and teams who have become legends. It’s an inspiring and fun look at the dedication, teamwork, and passion that define college sports, all located in the city the NCAA calls home.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 700 W Washington St, Indianapolis, IN 46204. In White River State Park.

Opening Hours: Tuesday-Saturday: 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM.

Cost: Adults ~$7, Youth (6-18) ~$5.

Website: https://www.ncaahallofchampions.org/

Lucas Oil Stadium Tour

Go behind the scenes at the state-of-the-art home of the NFL's Indianapolis Colts. Lucas Oil Stadium, with its iconic retractable roof and massive north-facing window, is an architectural marvel. Guided tours offer a glimpse into areas normally off-limits to the public. You'll get to walk onto the field, check out a locker room, visit the press box, and learn about the stadium's unique design and sustainability features. It's a fascinating experience for football fans and anyone interested in modern stadium engineering.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 500 S Capitol Ave, Indianapolis, IN 46225. At the south end of downtown.

Opening Hours: Tours are offered on select weekdays. Check the online schedule as it varies greatly.

Cost: Around $15 per person.

Website: https://www.lucasoilstadium.com/tours/

Mass Ave Arts District

Massachusetts Avenue, or 'Mass Ave,' is one of Indy's most vibrant and eclectic neighborhoods. This diagonal five-block stretch is packed with independent boutiques, art galleries, unique restaurants, theaters, and lively bars. It's the perfect place for a leisurely stroll, window shopping, and people-watching. The area has a distinct, artsy character, with colorful murals and public art installations scattered throughout. You can catch a show at the Old National Centre, browse contemporary art, find a unique souvenir, or enjoy a craft cocktail at a trendy bar. It’s a hub of local culture and a great place to feel the creative pulse of the city.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Runs diagonally northeast from downtown. Centered around the 400-800 blocks of Massachusetts Ave.

Opening Hours: Shops generally open 11 AM - 7 PM. Restaurants and bars open later.

Cost: Free to explore.

Website: https://www.massaveindy.org/

Fountain Square

Just southeast of downtown, Fountain Square is a quirky, retro neighborhood with a distinctive vintage flair. The area is a designated cultural district known for its independent restaurants, art galleries, live music venues, and unique entertainment options. The historic Fountain Square Theatre Building is the centerpiece, housing a boutique hotel, a duckpin bowling alley, and swing dancing events. This neighborhood is a fantastic destination for an evening out. You can enjoy dinner at a diverse range of eateries, see a burlesque show, play vintage arcade games, or listen to local bands. It offers a fun and offbeat alternative to the more mainstream downtown scene.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at the intersection of Virginia Ave, Shelby St, and Prospect St. A short drive or bus ride from downtown.

Opening Hours: Varies by establishment. Most active in the evenings and on weekends.

Cost: Free to walk around.

Website: https://www.discoverfountainsquare.com/

Conner Prairie

Step back in time at Conner Prairie, a nationally acclaimed living history museum just north of Indianapolis in Fishers. This unique attraction offers immersive experiences across different historical eras. You can explore the 1836 Prairietown, where costumed interpreters bring the 19th-century village to life, or visit the Lenape Indian Camp to learn about the area's original inhabitants. Other attractions include an 1859 Balloon Voyage (weather permitting), a Civil War journey, and an animal encounters barn. It's an engaging, educational, and fun experience for all ages, set on over 1,000 wooded acres. Conner Prairie makes history tangible and exciting, allowing you to participate in it rather than just observe.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 13400 Allisonville Rd, Fishers, IN 46038. About a 30-minute drive north of downtown Indianapolis.

Opening Hours: Typically April-October, Tuesday-Sunday 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM. Check for seasonal hours.

Cost: Adults ~$22, Youth (2-12) ~$17.

Website: https://www.connerprairie.org/

Indianapolis Zoo

Located within White River State Park, the Indianapolis Zoo is unique for being accredited as a zoo, aquarium, and botanical garden. The zoo is organized into biomes, allowing you to journey from the African plains to the Australian outback and the Amazonian rainforest in a single day. Highlights include the impressive orangutan center, a large dolphin pavilion with daily presentations, and the opportunity to touch sharks. The White River Gardens, included with admission, features a beautiful conservatory and outdoor themed gardens. The zoo's commitment to conservation and its well-designed habitats make it a top-tier attraction for animal lovers.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 1200 W Washington St, Indianapolis, IN 46222. Located in White River State Park.

Opening Hours: Daily, hours vary seasonally but generally 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM in summer.

Cost: Prices vary by day (~$20-$35). Book online in advance for better rates.

Website: https://www.indianapoliszoo.com/

Kurt Vonnegut Museum and Library

Celebrate the life and work of one of Indianapolis's most famous native sons, the author Kurt Vonnegut. This unique museum and library is a tribute to his literary genius, sharp wit, and humanist values. The collection features Vonnegut's personal typewriter, his Purple Heart medal, rejection letters, and first editions of his iconic novels like 'Slaughterhouse-Five.' The exhibits are both poignant and humorous, much like Vonnegut's writing. It's more than a museum; it's a cultural center that champions free speech, art, and critical thinking. It offers a deeply personal look at the man behind the books and is an essential stop for literary fans and free-thinkers.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 543 Indiana Ave, Indianapolis, IN 46202. Located on the historic Indiana Avenue.

Opening Hours: Wednesday-Monday, 12:00 PM - 5:00 PM. Closed Tuesdays.

Cost: Adults ~$12, Students ~$8.

Website: https://www.vonnegutlibrary.org/

Crown Hill Cemetery

A cemetery might seem like an unusual tourist destination, but Crown Hill is anything but ordinary. As the third-largest non-governmental cemetery in the nation, it is a place of history, art, and natural beauty. It sits on 555 rolling acres and offers one of the best panoramic views of the downtown skyline from 'Crown Hill,' the highest point in the county. This historic cemetery is the final resting place of many famous figures, including President Benjamin Harrison, poet James Whitcomb Riley, and notorious bank robber John Dillinger. You can take a guided tour or explore the beautiful grounds and elaborate monuments on your own. It's a peaceful and surprisingly fascinating place to spend an afternoon.

Practical Information

How to Get There: 700 W 38th St, Indianapolis, IN 46208. A short drive north of downtown.

Opening Hours: Grounds are open daily, 8:00 AM - 8:00 PM (seasonal variations).

Cost: Free to enter and explore.

Website: https://crownhill.org/

Planning Your Visit to Indianapolis

Getting Around

Downtown Indianapolis is very walkable, with many key attractions clustered together. For longer distances, the IndyGo bus system provides extensive coverage. Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are readily available. The Indiana Pacers Bikeshare program offers a fun and easy way to explore the city center and cultural trails, with numerous stations available.

Where to Stay

For first-time visitors, Downtown is the most convenient area, putting you within walking distance of Monument Circle, White River State Park, and Lucas Oil Stadium. For a trendier vibe with great restaurants and boutiques, stay near the Mass Ave Arts District. Broad Ripple Village, further north, is known for its vibrant nightlife and more residential feel. Hotels range from luxury options like the Conrad Indianapolis to reliable mid-range chains and budget-friendly choices.

Best Time to Visit

The best times to visit Indianapolis are spring (April-May) and fall (September-October). The weather is pleasant, perfect for walking the Canal and exploring the parks. May is an exciting but busy month due to the Indy 500 festivities. Summer (June-August) is warm and humid but hosts many outdoor concerts and events. Winter (November-March) is cold and can be snowy, but the holiday 'Circle of Lights' is a magical sight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Indianapolis expensive?
Indianapolis is generally considered an affordable city, especially compared to major coastal hubs like New York or Los Angeles. Costs for accommodation, dining, and attractions are very reasonable. You can find excellent meals for under $20, and many of the city's best experiences, like walking the cultural districts or parks, are free. While ticket prices for major sporting events can be high, overall travel costs are manageable.
How many days do you need in Indianapolis?
A long weekend of 2 to 3 days is perfect for a first-time visitor to Indianapolis. This allows enough time to see the main highlights, including the Speedway, a few museums in White River State Park, Monument Circle, and one of the cultural districts like Mass Ave or Fountain Square. If you're a big sports fan or want to explore more in-depth, you could easily extend your trip to 4 or 5 days.
Is Indianapolis safe?
Like any major American city, Indianapolis has areas with higher crime rates, but the downtown core and tourist-centric areas are generally very safe. Areas like Monument Circle, White River State Park, Mass Ave, and the Convention Center area are well-patrolled and accustomed to visitors. It's always wise to practice standard urban safety precautions: be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, don't leave valuables in your car, and stick to well-lit areas.
What is Indianapolis famous for?
Indianapolis is most famous for the Indianapolis 500, the world's largest single-day sporting event. It's a major hub for amateur sports, serving as the headquarters for the NCAA. The city is also known as the 'Crossroads of America' due to its central location and convergence of several major interstate highways. Additionally, it has more monuments and memorials dedicated to veterans than any other U.S. city outside of Washington, D.C., and is known for its warm 'Hoosier Hospitality'.
