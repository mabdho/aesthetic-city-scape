15 Best Things to Do in Bilbao, Spain (2025 Guide)

Once an industrial powerhouse, Bilbao has undergone a breathtaking transformation into a global hub for art, architecture, and gastronomy. Nestled in the heart of Basque Country, this vibrant city captivates visitors with its seamless blend of avant-garde design and old-world charm. The shimmering titanium curves of the Guggenheim Museum are just the beginning; beyond it lies a city teeming with life. Explore the labyrinthine streets of the Casco Viejo, savor world-renowned pintxos in bustling bars, and ride a historic funicular for sweeping views of the green hills that cradle the city. Whether you're an art aficionado, a dedicated foodie, or simply seeking a unique urban escape, you'll find an incredible array of experiences. This guide will walk you through the very best things to do in Bilbao, ensuring your visit is nothing short of unforgettable.

Complete Guide to Bilbao Attractions

Guggenheim Museum Bilbao

An architectural marvel that single-handedly redefined Bilbao's global image. Designed by Frank Gehry, the museum's swirling, titanium-clad form is as much a masterpiece as the art it contains. The building seems to change its skin with the shifting light, reflecting the sky and the adjacent Nervión River. Inside, you'll find a vast collection of modern and contemporary art, featuring large-scale, site-specific installations by artists like Richard Serra and Jeff Koons. Don't miss the iconic floral sculpture 'Puppy' by Jeff Koons guarding the entrance, or Louise Bourgeois's towering spider, 'Maman'. The Guggenheim is not just a museum; it's a transformative experience and the undisputed heart of modern Bilbao.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at Abandoibarra Etorb., 2. Easily accessible on foot from the city center or via the 'Guggenheim' tram stop.

Opening Hours: Tuesday to Sunday: 10:00 AM - 7:00 PM. Closed on Mondays (except some holidays). Check website for seasonal changes.

Cost: Adults approx. €13-€16. Discounts for students and seniors. It's recommended to book tickets online in advance.

Website: https://www.guggenheim-bilbao.eus/

Casco Viejo (Old Town)

Known as 'Las Siete Calles' or 'The Seven Streets', this is the medieval heart of Bilbao. A delightful maze of narrow, pedestrian-only streets, the Casco Viejo is packed with independent shops, historic churches, and some of the city's best pintxos bars. It's an atmospheric district where centuries of history feel alive. The central hub is Plaza Nueva, a beautiful neoclassical square perfect for a coffee or a round of pintxos. As you wander, you'll stumble upon landmarks like the Santiago Cathedral and the Basque Museum, making it a perfect area for an afternoon of aimless exploration and culinary discovery.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Accessible via the 'Casco Viejo' metro station, which serves all three metro lines. It's also a short walk from the Abando district.

Opening Hours: Streets are always open. Shops typically open from 10:00 AM - 2:00 PM and 5:00 PM - 8:00 PM. Bars and restaurants have longer hours.

Cost: Free to explore.

Website: N/A

Mercado de la Ribera

Perched on the bank of the Nervión River, the Mercado de la Ribera is officially the largest covered market in Europe. This Art Deco gem, dating back to 1929, is a feast for the senses. The ground floor is a traditional market where locals shop for the freshest fish, meat, cheese, and produce from the Basque countryside. The upper floor has been transformed into a modern gastro-market. Here, you can sample an incredible variety of pintxos, local wines, and craft beers from numerous stalls, often accompanied by live music. It's the perfect place to experience Bilbao's culinary soul in a lively, authentic setting.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at the edge of Casco Viejo, right next to the 'Ribera' tram stop.

Opening Hours: Market stalls: Mon-Fri 8:00 AM - 2:30 PM & 5:00 PM - 8:00 PM, Sat 8:00 AM - 3:00 PM. Gastro bars have longer hours, typically until 11:00 PM or later.

Cost: Free to enter. Cost of food and drink varies.

Website: https://www.mercadodelaribera.eus/

Vizcaya Bridge (Puente Colgante)

A remarkable feat of 19th-century engineering and a UNESCO World Heritage site, the Vizcaya Bridge is the world's oldest transporter bridge. Opened in 1893, it connects the towns of Portugalete and Las Arenas (part of Getxo) across the Nervión estuary. You can experience it in two ways: ride the 'gondola' (a hanging transporter cabin) across with cars and pedestrians for a nominal fee, or take the lift up to the 50-meter-high walkway. The walkway offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Abra Bay and the coastline. It’s a testament to the region's industrial heritage and an absolute must-see for anyone interested in history and engineering.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take Metro Line 1 to 'Areeta' (for Las Arenas side) or Line 2 to 'Portugalete' (for Portugalete side). The journey takes about 20 minutes from central Bilbao.

Opening Hours: Gondola runs 24/7. Walkway is open daily, typically from 10:00 AM to dusk.

Cost: Gondola ride is under €1. Walkway access is approx. €9.

Website: https://puente-colgante.com/

Mount Artxanda Funicular

For the best panoramic view of Bilbao, a trip up Mount Artxanda is essential. A charming red funicular railway, in operation since 1915, whisks you up the steep hillside in just three minutes. From the summit, you're rewarded with a stunning vista of the entire city, framed by rolling green hills. You can clearly see the Guggenheim's intricate design, the winding Nervión River, and the dense layout of the Casco Viejo. There's a park at the top, along with a few restaurants known for their traditional Basque cooking, making it an ideal spot for a leisurely lunch or a spectacular sunset photo session.

Practical Information

How to Get There: The lower station is at Funikularreko Plaza, a 15-minute walk from the Guggenheim or Zubizuri Bridge.

Opening Hours: Daily, typically from 7:15 AM to 10:00 PM (11:00 PM in summer), with trains every 15 minutes.

Cost: Approx. €4.50 for a round trip. Discounted with the Barik card.

Website: https://funicularartxanda.bilbao.eus/

Museo de Bellas Artes (Fine Arts Museum)

While the Guggenheim often steals the spotlight, Bilbao's Fine Arts Museum is a world-class institution in its own right and a favorite among locals. Its impressive collection spans from the 12th century to the present day, with a particular focus on Spanish and Basque artists like El Greco, Goya, Sorolla, and Zuloaga. The museum is cleverly organized into three sections: classical art, contemporary art, and Basque art. This chronological and thematic journey offers a deep dive into the region's artistic heritage, providing a rich contrast to the contemporary works at the Guggenheim.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Doña Casilda Iturrizar Park, a short walk from the Guggenheim. The 'Euskalduna' tram stop is nearby.

Opening Hours: Wednesday to Monday: 10:00 AM - 8:00 PM. Closed on Tuesdays.

Cost: Adults approx. €12. Free entry on Wednesdays.

Website: https://www.museobilbao.com/

Zubizuri Bridge

Designed by the famous Spanish architect Santiago Calatrava, the Zubizuri (Basque for 'white bridge') is a striking, sail-like footbridge that arches gracefully over the Nervión River. Its elegant white steel structure and translucent glass brick walkway make it a popular spot for photographers. Though it faced controversy over its slippery surface (now covered by a non-slip mat), it remains a symbol of the new, design-forward Bilbao. It provides a beautiful pedestrian link between the city's Ensanche district and the area leading to the Artxanda Funicular, offering lovely views of the riverfront promenade.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Centrally located on the Nervión River, between the Guggenheim Museum and the City Hall.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7.

Cost: Free to cross.

Website: N/A

Plaza Nueva

The social heart of the Casco Viejo, Plaza Nueva is a magnificent neoclassical square enclosed by arcaded buildings. Built in the mid-19th century, its uniform arches and grand scale provide a sense of calm and order amidst the bustling narrow streets of the Old Town. Underneath the arcades, you'll find a fantastic concentration of bars and restaurants, each vying for the title of best pintxos. On Sundays, the square hosts a lively flea market where you can browse for old books, coins, and antiques. It's the quintessential place to participate in the local 'txikiteo' (pintxos crawl).

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the center of the Casco Viejo, easily found by walking from any of the 'Seven Streets'.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7. Bar and restaurant hours vary.

Cost: Free to visit.

Website: N/A

Azkuna Zentroa (Alhóndiga)

A brilliant example of urban regeneration, Azkuna Zentroa is a former municipal wine warehouse transformed into a multi-purpose cultural and leisure center. The industrial exterior belies the fantastical interior, reimagined by French designer Philippe Starck. Inside, a vast atrium is supported by 43 uniquely designed pillars, each representing a different culture and moment in history. The center houses cinemas, a library, exhibition halls, a gym, and a stunning rooftop swimming pool with a glass bottom, visible from the atrium below. It's a vibrant public space where art, culture, and community converge.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the Indautxu district. The 'Indautxu' or 'Moyua' metro stops are a 5-minute walk away.

Opening Hours: Daily, typically from 7:00 AM to 11:00 PM. Individual facility hours vary.

Cost: Free to enter and explore the main atrium.

Website: https://www.azkunazentroa.eus/

Santiago Cathedral

Dedicated to the apostle Saint James, this beautiful Gothic cathedral is the oldest building in Bilbao and a key stop on the coastal route of the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage. Built primarily in the 14th and 15th centuries, it features a serene interior, a lovely cloister, and an impressive altarpiece. While its exterior was heavily restored in the 19th century, the cathedral retains its spiritual and historical significance. Located deep within the Casco Viejo, its quiet atmosphere offers a peaceful respite from the lively streets outside.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the heart of the Casco Viejo, a short walk from the 'Casco Viejo' metro station.

Opening Hours: Daily, generally 10:00 AM - 7:00 PM. Check for service times.

Cost: Entry is approx. €6, which includes an audio guide.

Website: https://catedralbilbao.com/

Doña Casilda Iturrizar Park

Bilbao's green lung, this beautiful park offers a tranquil escape from the urban hustle. Designed in an English romantic style, it features a duck pond, fountains, tree-lined promenades, and a classical-style carousel. It's a favorite spot for locals to stroll, jog, or relax on a bench. The park is also home to the Museo de Bellas Artes, creating a perfect combination of nature and culture. Its proximity to the Guggenheim and the Euskalduna Conference Centre makes it a central and accessible green space.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located between the districts of Abando and Indautxu. The 'Euskalduna' tram stop is right next to it.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7.

Cost: Free.

Website: N/A

Itsasmuseum Bilbao (Maritime Museum)

Situated on the grounds of the former Euskalduna shipyards, the Itsasmuseum explores Bilbao's profound connection to the sea, the estuary, and shipbuilding. The exhibits trace the region's maritime history, from whaling to the industrial age of iron ore exportation. Outside, in the old docks, you can see a collection of historic vessels, including the 'Casa de Bombas' (pump house) and cranes that are remnants of the shipyard. It’s a fascinating look into the industrial past that funded Bilbao's modern renaissance.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Next to the Euskalduna Conference Centre. The 'Euskalduna' tram stop is the closest public transport option.

Opening Hours: Tuesday to Sunday: 10:00 AM - 8:00 PM. Closed Mondays.

Cost: Adults approx. €6. Free entry on Tuesdays after 3:00 PM.

Website: https://www.itsasmuseum.eus/

San Mamés Stadium

Football is a religion in Bilbao, and the San Mamés Stadium is its cathedral. Home to Athletic Club Bilbao, a team famous for its policy of only fielding players of Basque origin, the stadium is an icon of local pride. Even if you can't catch a match, the stadium tour and museum are highly recommended. You'll get a behind-the-scenes look at the press room, changing rooms, and the pitch itself. The museum showcases the club's storied 125-year history. Known as 'The Cathedral of Football,' the atmosphere here on match day is legendary.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the metro to 'San Mamés' station, which is directly connected to the stadium.

Opening Hours: Museum & Tour: Tuesday to Sunday, 10:00 AM - 7:00 PM. Hours vary on match days.

Cost: Tour & Museum entry is approx. €14.

Website: https://www.athletic-club.eus/museoa

Explore Getxo and its Beaches

A short metro ride from Bilbao, the elegant seaside town of Getxo offers a complete change of pace. It's famous for its grand 19th-century villas built by wealthy industrialists, particularly in the Las Arenas and Neguri neighborhoods. Start at the Vizcaya Bridge and then enjoy a long walk or bike ride along the coastal path. You'll pass by the charming old port of Algorta and reach beautiful sandy beaches like Playa de Ereaga and Playa de Arrigunaga, which are popular with surfers and sunbathers. It's the perfect half-day or full-day trip to experience the Basque coastline.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take Metro Line 1 towards Plentzia. Get off at 'Areeta' for the bridge, 'Algorta' for the old port, or 'Bidezabal' for the beaches.

Opening Hours: Always accessible.

Cost: Free to explore, plus the cost of the metro ticket.

Website: https://www.getxo.eus/en/turismo

Go Pintxo Hopping

More than just an activity, pintxo hopping (or 'txikiteo') is a cornerstone of Basque social life and a culinary art form. Unlike Spanish tapas, pintxos are often more elaborate, served on a slice of bread and held together with a 'pincho' (cocktail stick). The best way to experience it is to hop from bar to bar, especially in the Casco Viejo or the Indautxu district. In each bar, order one or two pintxos and a small drink (a 'zurito' of beer or a 'txakoli' of local white wine). Don't be shy; point at what looks good on the bar, enjoy it standing up with the locals, and then move on to the next spot.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Best areas are Casco Viejo (especially Plaza Nueva) and Calle Diputación in the Abando district.

Opening Hours: Pintxos are best around lunchtime (1:00 PM - 3:00 PM) and early evening (7:00 PM - 10:00 PM).

Cost: Pintxos typically cost between €2 and €5 each.

Website: N/A

Planning Your Visit to Bilbao

Getting Around

Bilbao is a very walkable city, especially the central areas. For longer distances, the public transport system is excellent. The Metro Bilbao is fast and efficient, connecting the city center with the coast. The Euskotren tram is perfect for scenic riverside routes, hitting key spots like the Guggenheim and Casco Viejo. Local buses (Bilbobus) cover the rest of the city. A 'Barik Card' is a rechargeable travel card that offers cheaper fares across all transport options and is highly recommended.

Where to Stay

For first-time visitors, Abando is the best area. It's central, safe, and home to the Guggenheim, luxury shops, and great restaurants. Hotels like Gran Hotel Domine Bilbao offer incredible views. For historic charm and lively nightlife, stay in Casco Viejo (Old Town), but be aware it can be noisy. Indautxu is a stylish residential area, great for shopping and dining, with excellent hotels like Hotel Carlton. For budget travelers, there are several modern hostels scattered throughout the city.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Bilbao is during the spring (May-June) and early autumn (September-October). During these months, the weather is pleasantly warm and sunny, perfect for exploring, and the city is less crowded than in peak summer. July and August are the warmest months but also the busiest. A highlight of the summer is Aste Nagusia ('The Great Week') in late August, a massive nine-day festival with concerts, fireworks, and street entertainment.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: Art & River. Start at the Guggenheim Museum (book tickets ahead). Afterwards, walk along the river, crossing the Zubizuri Bridge. Visit the Museo de Bellas Artes. In the evening, go for pintxos in the chic Indautxu district. Day 2: Old Town & Views. Explore the seven streets of Casco Viejo, visit Santiago Cathedral, and get lost in the Mercado de la Ribera for lunch. In the late afternoon, take the Funicular de Artxanda for stunning sunset views over the city. Day 3: Coast & Culture. Take the metro to Portugalete to see the Vizcaya Bridge. Cross on the hanging gondola to Getxo and walk along the coast to see the grand villas and beaches. Return to Bilbao and spend the evening exploring the Azkuna Zentroa cultural center.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bilbao expensive?
Bilbao is moderately priced compared to other major Spanish cities like Madrid or Barcelona. Accommodation and museum entry fees are on par with European standards. However, daily expenses can be quite reasonable. Eating out is a great value, especially if you embrace the pintxos culture, where you can have a filling and delicious meal for €15-€20. Public transport is also affordable, especially with a Barik card.
How many days do you need in Bilbao?
Two to three full days are ideal for a first visit to Bilbao. This gives you enough time to see the main attractions like the Guggenheim Museum, explore the Casco Viejo, enjoy the views from Artxanda, and indulge in plenty of pintxos without feeling rushed. If you want to take a day trip to the coast (like Getxo or San Juan de Gaztelugatxe), plan for three to four days.
Is Bilbao safe?
Bilbao is a very safe city, with low crime rates. You can feel comfortable walking around most areas, even at night. As in any major city, standard precautions apply. Be mindful of your belongings in crowded places like the Casco Viejo or on public transport to avoid petty theft or pickpocketing. The Basque Country in general is one of the safest regions in Spain.
What is Bilbao famous for?
Bilbao is most famous for the Guggenheim Museum, an architectural icon that sparked the city's revitalization. It's also renowned for its world-class Basque cuisine, particularly pintxos, which are a culinary art form here. The city is also known for its incredible transformation from a gritty industrial port to a polished cultural hub, its historic Casco Viejo, and its passionate football club, Athletic Club Bilbao.
