15 Best Things to Do in Port Moresby, Papua New Guinea (2025 Guide)

As the vibrant capital of Papua New Guinea, Port Moresby is a city of compelling contrasts, where modern development meets ancient traditions on the edge of a stunning natural harbour. Often serving as a gateway to the country's remote islands and untamed jungles, the city itself holds a wealth of cultural treasures and natural beauty. From sprawling nature parks teeming with unique wildlife to poignant war memorials and bustling local markets, there is a surprising array of experiences waiting to be discovered. For any traveler looking to understand the heart of this diverse nation, exploring the rich tapestry of sights, sounds, and stories is essential. This guide will walk you through all the very best things to do in Port Moresby, ensuring a memorable and insightful journey into the Land of the Unexpected.

Complete Guide to Port Moresby Attractions

Port Moresby Nature Park

Port Moresby Nature Park is an essential first stop, offering a fantastic introduction to Papua New Guinea's incredible biodiversity in a safe and accessible setting. Formerly the National Botanic Gardens, this beautifully maintained park is home to over 550 native animals, including numerous species of Birds of Paradise, tree kangaroos, cassowaries, wallabies, and crocodiles. The park's well-signed pathways wind through lush rainforest settings, past vibrant orchid gardens, and into large walk-through aviaries that allow for up-close encounters with stunning birdlife. Beyond the wildlife, the park also features cultural displays, a children's playground, and a pleasant café. It's an excellent, family-friendly attraction that combines recreation, education, and conservation, providing a perfect overview of the natural wonders that make PNG so unique.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located next to the University of Papua New Guinea in the Waigani area. It's best reached by a pre-booked taxi or hotel transport.

Opening Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM - 4:30 PM

Cost: Approximately 20-25 PGK for residents, higher for non-residents. Check their website for current pricing.

Website: https://www.portmoresbynaturepark.org/

National Museum and Art Gallery

To truly appreciate the cultural depth of Papua New Guinea, a visit to the National Museum and Art Gallery is non-negotiable. This institution houses the nation's most important collection of cultural artifacts, representing the incredible diversity of the country's 800+ distinct cultures. The galleries showcase everything from intricately carved storyboards and ceremonial masks from the Sepik River region to elaborate headdresses from the Highlands, traditional canoes, and ancient pottery. The museum also features exhibits on the nation's natural history, archaeological finds, and contemporary art, providing a comprehensive overview of PNG's past and present. It's a quiet, reflective space that offers invaluable context for the rest of your journey through the country.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Waigani, near Parliament House. Easily accessible by taxi.

Opening Hours: Monday - Friday, 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM; Weekends vary. Check ahead of your visit.

Cost: Entry is typically free, but donations are welcome.

Website: https://www.museumpng.gov.pg/

Varirata National Park

Just a 45-minute drive from the city, Varirata National Park offers a refreshing escape into nature and a chance to experience PNG's famed birdwatching. As the country's first national park, it protects a swathe of savannah and rainforest along the Sogeri Plateau. The park has several well-marked walking trails of varying difficulty, leading to spectacular lookout points with panoramic views over Port Moresby and the coastline. The main attraction for many is the birdlife, particularly the opportunity to spot the Raggiana Bird of Paradise, PNG's national bird. The best time for birding is early in the morning. Even if you're not an avid birder, the cool air, lush scenery, and stunning vistas make it a worthwhile excursion.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on the Sogeri Road. It's highly recommended to hire a driver or join a guided tour for the day. Self-driving is not advised for first-time visitors.

Opening Hours: Daily, 6:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Cost: An entrance fee is charged per person and per vehicle.

Website: N/A - Information available through tour operators.

Bomana War Cemetery

A deeply moving and immaculately maintained site, the Bomana War Cemetery is the final resting place for over 3,800 Allied soldiers, sailors, and airmen who died during the campaigns in New Guinea during World War II. The majority of those interred here died on the Kokoda Track. The rows of white headstones are set on a beautiful, peaceful lawn that slopes down a hill, creating a powerful and poignant atmosphere. Reading the inscriptions and taking in the scale of the sacrifice is a sobering experience that provides a crucial historical perspective on the region. It is the largest war cemetery in the Pacific and is managed by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission. A visit here is a gesture of respect and remembrance.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located about 19 km north of Port Moresby. A taxi or guided tour is the best way to visit.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7, but best visited during daylight hours.

Cost: Free

Website: https://www.cwgc.org/visit-us/find-cemeteries-memorials/cemetery-details/2010100/bomana-war-cemetery/

Parliament House

The National Parliament House is one of Port Moresby's most iconic buildings and a symbol of the nation's independence and unity. Opened in 1984, its design is a stunning blend of modern architecture and traditional motifs, drawing inspiration from both the spirit houses of the Sepik region and the roundhouses of the Highlands. The main facade is adorned with a spectacular mosaic depicting various cultural themes. While access to the interior may be restricted when parliament is in session, it's possible to arrange guided tours at other times. Even viewing the building from the outside is impressive and offers fantastic photo opportunities. It stands as a proud monument to PNG's rich cultural heritage and its modern political identity.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the government district of Waigani. Easily reached by taxi.

Opening Hours: Exterior can be viewed anytime. For interior tours, it's best to inquire in advance through official channels or a tour operator.

Cost: Free to view from the outside.

Website: http://www.parliament.gov.pg/

Ela Beach

Once the primary recreational area for colonial residents, Ela Beach has been beautifully redeveloped into a modern public space. A wide, clean, and sandy stretch, it is now a popular spot for locals and expats to walk, jog, and relax, especially in the late afternoons. The beachfront is lined with walking paths, sports facilities including basketball and volleyball courts, and shaded picnic areas. The area also hosts a craft market on the last Saturday of each month, where you can buy authentic, high-quality handicrafts directly from artisans from across the country. It's a great place to observe daily life in Port Moresby and enjoy the sea breeze.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located adjacent to the downtown area (Town). Easily accessible by foot from nearby hotels or by a short taxi ride.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7, but safest to visit during the day and early evening.

Cost: Free

Website: N/A

Owers' Corner & Kokoda Track Start

For history buffs and adventure seekers, a trip to Owers' Corner is a pilgrimage. This location marks the southern start/end point of the legendary Kokoda Track, the 96-kilometer trail that was the site of a brutal campaign between Australian and Japanese forces in WWII. While hiking the full track is a serious multi-day endeavor, you can visit Owers' Corner on a day trip from Port Moresby. At the site, you can see memorials, learn about the history, and even walk a short section of the track down to the Goldie River to get a small taste of the challenging terrain faced by the soldiers. The drive itself, up through the Sogeri Plateau, is scenic and provides a glimpse into the rugged interior of the country.

Practical Information

How to Get There: About a 1.5-hour drive from Port Moresby. A guided tour or a private driver is essential as the road can be rough.

Opening Hours: Accessible during daylight hours.

Cost: No fee for the site, but transport and guide costs apply.

Website: Information available through Kokoda tour companies like Kokoda Track Authority.

Hanuabada Village

Experience a unique way of life at Hanuabada, which means 'Great Village' in the Motu language. This is one of the oldest and largest stilt villages in the Pacific, with houses built on wooden stilts over the waters of Port Moresby Harbour. It's the historical home of the Motuan people, the original inhabitants of the area. Walking along the wooden boardwalks gives you an intimate look at a community that has lived this way for generations. It is crucial to visit with a local guide who has connections to the community. This ensures your visit is respectful and welcomed. A guide can help facilitate interactions and share the history of the village, including its role in the Hiri Moale trading voyages.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on the northwestern edge of the downtown area. A guided tour is the only recommended way to visit.

Opening Hours: By arrangement with a guide, during daylight hours.

Cost: Varies depending on the guide or tour.

Website: N/A - Arrange through a reputable local tour operator.

Loloata Island Resort

For a touch of luxury and a fantastic marine escape, Loloata Island Resort is a premier destination. Located a short boat ride from the mainland, this private island offers pristine beaches, world-class diving and snorkeling, and upscale accommodation. The surrounding waters are part of a protected marine sanctuary, boasting vibrant coral reefs and a rich diversity of sea life, including several shipwrecks accessible to divers. Even if you're not staying overnight, you can often purchase a day pass which includes boat transfers, lunch, and use of the resort facilities like the swimming pools and beach. It's the perfect way to relax, swim, and experience PNG's stunning underwater world in a comfortable and secure environment.

Practical Information

How to Get There: The resort's jetty is a 20-minute drive from the airport. The resort arranges boat transfers from their mainland jetty.

Opening Hours: Open to guests 24/7. Day passes subject to availability.

Cost: Accommodation is high-end. Day pass prices vary, check directly with the resort.

Website: https://www.loloataislandresort.com/

Adventure Park PNG

A great option for families, Adventure Park PNG offers a diverse range of activities in one location. It combines a small zoo, a water park with slides, an orchid garden, and even a Ferris wheel. The animal section features many of PNG's iconic species, and it's particularly famous for its daily crocodile feeding show. The park provides a more recreational and less formal atmosphere than the Port Moresby Nature Park, making it a hit with children. It's a place to have some lighthearted fun and cool off on a hot day, while still getting a glimpse of local wildlife and flora.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at 14 Mile, outside the city center on the Sogeri Road. Best reached by taxi or private car.

Opening Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Cost: Entrance fee applies, with additional costs for some rides.

Website: N/A - Information often found on their social media pages.

Royal Port Moresby Golf Club

For golf enthusiasts, the Royal Port Moresby Golf Club offers a chance to play a round on a well-regarded 18-hole championship course. Established in the 1950s, the club has a rich history and provides a green oasis within the city. The course is known for being challenging yet enjoyable, with lush fairways lined by tropical vegetation. The club is welcoming to international visitors, offering equipment rental and access to its clubhouse facilities, which include a restaurant and bar. It's a great way to spend a morning or afternoon, meet some local and expatriate residents, and enjoy a sporting activity in a secure environment.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Boroko, a central suburb of Port Moresby. Accessible by taxi.

Opening Hours: Generally open from sunrise to sunset. Contact the club for visitor tee times.

Cost: Green fees apply for non-members. Contact the club for current rates.

Website: https://www.rpromgc.com.pg/

Vision City Mega Mall

To see the modern face of Port Moresby, spend some time at Vision City Mega Mall. As the largest shopping mall in Papua New Guinea, it's a hub of contemporary life in the capital. Here you'll find a wide range of shops, a multi-screen cinema, a large supermarket, and a diverse food court offering both international and local fast-food options. While not a traditional cultural attraction, it offers a fascinating insight into the city's growing middle class and urban culture. It's also a comfortable, air-conditioned, and secure place to shop for supplies, grab a meal, or simply people-watch.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Waigani. Almost every taxi driver knows its location.

Opening Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM - 9:00 PM (individual store hours may vary).

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: https://visioncity.com.pg/

Burns Peak

For one of the best panoramic views of Port Moresby, head up to Burns Peak. This lookout point offers a stunning vista over the downtown area, Ela Beach, the harbour, and the surrounding suburbs. It's a particularly beautiful spot at sunrise or sunset, when the light casts a golden glow over the city and the sea. Safety is paramount for this attraction. Do not attempt to hike or drive up alone. The only recommended way to visit is as part of an organized tour with a reputable operator or with a trusted local guide who is familiar with the area. When done safely, the reward is an unparalleled perspective of the city's layout and natural setting.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the hills behind the main town area. Only visit with a secure, organized tour.

Opening Hours: Best at sunrise or sunset; depends on your tour operator.

Cost: Cost of the guided tour.

Website: N/A - Arrange through tour operators like Paga Hill Development.

Idler's Beach

Located about 20 kilometers west of the city, Idler's Beach is a popular weekend getaway for Port Moresby residents. It's a private beach that requires a small entry fee, which helps maintain its cleanliness and security. The beach offers a long stretch of white sand and calm, clear waters that are perfect for swimming and snorkeling. Basic facilities like changing rooms and barbecue areas are available. It's a simple, no-frills destination, but its appeal lies in its relaxed atmosphere and safe environment, making it a great choice for a day of sun and sea away from the city's hustle.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Accessible via the road towards the LNG plant. A private car or hired taxi for the day is necessary.

Opening Hours: Typically open on weekends, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM. Check locally for current status.

Cost: An entrance fee is charged per person and per vehicle.

Website: N/A

Taurama Beach

Taurama Beach holds both recreational and historical significance. Located to the east of the city, this beach is known for its distinctive black sand and is a popular spot for locals. It's also an important archaeological site, with evidence of human settlement dating back over 2,000 years, connecting it to the early Lapita and Motuan cultures. Nearby Taurama Barracks (a military base) means the area has a degree of security, but like any non-tourist beach, it's best to visit with a local contact or guide. It offers a more authentic beach experience than the more developed spots and a connection to the deep history of the region.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located east of the city, near the army barracks. A taxi or private car is needed.

Opening Hours: Daylight hours are recommended.

Cost: Free

Website: N/A

Planning Your Visit to Port Moresby

Getting Around

Navigating Port Moresby requires careful planning. The safest and most reliable options for tourists are hotel-arranged transport, pre-booked taxis from reputable companies, or hiring a car with a local driver. Ride-sharing apps are not prevalent. While Public Motor Vehicles (PMVs) are the most common form of local transport, they are generally not recommended for visitors due to safety concerns and overcrowding. For most attractions, arranging transport through your hotel or a tour operator is the best approach.

Where to Stay

The best areas for visitors are the secure precincts of Downtown (Town) and Waigani. Downtown offers proximity to Ela Beach and the harbour. Waigani is near the government buildings, museums, and malls. Top hotel recommendations known for their security and amenities include The Stanley Hotel & Suites, Grand Papua Hotel, Hilton Port Moresby, and Airways Hotel, which often feature in lists of the world's best airport hotels.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Port Moresby is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. During these months, you can expect less rainfall, lower humidity, and more sunshine, making it ideal for outdoor activities like hiking in Varirata National Park or visiting the beaches. The wet season (November to April) brings heavy rains and higher humidity.

Suggested Itinerary

A simple 3-day itinerary: Day 1: Culture & History. Start at the National Museum and Art Gallery to gain cultural context. In the afternoon, visit the iconic Parliament House (exterior) and the poignant Bomana War Cemetery. Day 2: Nature & Wildlife. Spend the morning at the Port Moresby Nature Park to see PNG's unique fauna. In the afternoon, take a guided trip to Varirata National Park for stunning views and birdwatching. Day 3: Local Life & Relaxation. Take a guided tour of Hanuabada Stilt Village in the morning. In the afternoon, relax at Ela Beach or arrange a day pass for a marine escape to Loloata Island Resort.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Port Moresby expensive?
Port Moresby can be quite expensive for travelers. Accommodation in secure, high-end hotels is comparable to prices in major Western cities. Guided tours, private drivers, and flights are also significant expenses. Imported goods and dining in hotels and upscale restaurants are costly. You can save money by eating at local markets or food courts in malls like Vision City, but generally, you should budget more than you would for a trip to Southeast Asia.
How many days do you need in Port Moresby?
To cover the main highlights of Port Moresby and its immediate surroundings, 3 to 4 days is sufficient. This allows enough time for the major cultural sites, a nature park, a historical tour, and some relaxation. If you are using Port Moresby as a base for longer excursions, such as starting the Kokoda Track or flying to other provinces, you will need to factor in additional time.
Is Port Moresby safe?
Port Moresby has a serious reputation for crime, and visitors must prioritize their safety. It is not a city where you can casually walk around, especially after dark or alone. However, a safe trip is possible by taking sensible precautions. Always use pre-arranged transport from reputable sources (like your hotel), avoid displaying valuables, be aware of your surroundings, and do not travel to unfamiliar areas without a trusted local guide. Staying at one of the major, secure hotels significantly enhances safety. By being cautious and well-prepared, you can navigate the city and its attractions safely.
What is Port Moresby famous for?
Port Moresby is famous for being the capital and largest city of the culturally diverse nation of Papua New Guinea. It is known for its large natural harbor, its unique blend of modern and traditional architecture like the Parliament House, and as the gateway to the country's incredible natural wonders. Historically, it is famous as a key strategic location during World War II and for its proximity to the legendary Kokoda Track.
