15 Best Things to Do in Lusaka, Zambia (2025 Guide)

Welcome to Lusaka, the vibrant, sprawling heart of Zambia. Far more than just a transit hub for safari-goers, this city pulses with an infectious energy, blending rapid development with deep-rooted traditions. Here, bustling markets and contemporary art galleries sit alongside tranquil wildlife sanctuaries on the city's fringe. It's a city of contrasts, where business suits and colourful chitenge fabrics share the same sidewalk. From the poignant history enshrined in its national monuments to the thrilling sight of a rescued elephant calf, Lusaka offers a surprisingly diverse array of experiences. Exploring the capital provides a fascinating insight into modern Zambian life and reveals some of the most underrated attractions in Southern Africa. For any visitor, discovering the best things to do in Lusaka is an adventure in itself, promising memorable encounters with wildlife, culture, and the warm hospitality of the Zambian people.

Complete Guide to Lusaka Attractions

Lilayi Elephant Nursery

A truly heartwarming and essential stop, the Lilayi Elephant Nursery is a project by Game Rangers International (GRI) dedicated to rescuing and rehabilitating orphaned elephant calves. The nursery provides a safe haven where these vulnerable animals are cared for by dedicated keepers before their eventual re-integration into the wild in Kafue National Park. Visitors can watch the baby elephants during their daily feeding and mud bath from a special viewing platform. An informative talk from the GRI team explains the conservation challenges and the individual stories of the rescued elephants. It's an inspiring experience that directly supports vital conservation work and offers a unique opportunity to see these magnificent creatures up close in a responsible, ethical setting. The viewing is strictly timed to minimize stress on the animals, making it a well-managed and impactful visit.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on the property of Lilayi Lodge, about a 30-minute drive south of central Lusaka. Best reached by taxi or private car.

Opening Hours: Public viewing is strictly between 11:30 AM and 1:00 PM daily.

Cost: Entry by donation. A suggested donation of around 150-200 ZMW per person is appreciated.

Website: https://www.gamerangersinternational.org/lilayi-elephant-nursery

Lusaka National Park

Remarkably located just on the southern edge of the city, Lusaka National Park is Zambia's newest and most accessible national park. It offers a fantastic 'safari-in-the-city' experience, perfect for those with limited time. Despite its proximity to the urban sprawl, the park hosts an impressive variety of wildlife. You can spot giraffes, zebras, blue wildebeest, eland, and sable antelope. The park is also a designated sanctuary for the endangered white rhino, and seeing these majestic animals is a major highlight. With a network of well-maintained roads, the park is easily navigable by a standard vehicle or you can hire a game-viewing vehicle at the entrance. It's a perfect half-day trip to escape the city's hustle and immerse yourself in Zambia's beautiful miombo woodland landscape.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located off Leopards Hill Road in the south-eastern outskirts of Lusaka. Accessible by car or taxi.

Opening Hours: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM daily.

Cost: Approx. $5 USD for citizens, $10 for residents, $15 for international visitors. Vehicle fees also apply.

Website: https://www.zambiatourism.com/parks/lusaka-national-park/

Kabwata Cultural Village

For an authentic dose of Zambian culture and craftsmanship, a visit to Kabwata Cultural Village is a must. This living market is comprised of a series of thatched-roof huts where you can watch artisans at work, practicing traditional skills like wood carving, pottery, and weaving. It's one of the best places in Lusaka to buy high-quality, authentic souvenirs directly from the people who made them. You'll find everything from intricate wooden sculptures and masks to colourful textiles, tribal drums, and beaded jewellery. The atmosphere is relaxed, and the sellers are generally friendly and open to a bit of good-natured bargaining. It’s more than just a market; it’s a cultural center that actively preserves and showcases Zambia's rich artistic heritage.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Burma Road, a short taxi ride from the city center.

Opening Hours: Approximately 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM daily.

Cost: Free to enter; costs vary for purchases.

Website: N/A - Information available on general Zambian tourism sites.

Sunday Crafts Market

Every Sunday, the parking lot of Arcades Shopping Mall transforms into a vibrant, sprawling open-air market. This is the city's premier destination for crafts, souvenirs, and local art. It's significantly larger and offers a wider variety than Kabwata, featuring vendors from across Zambia and even neighbouring countries. You can find an incredible array of goods: copper items, Malawian chairs, paintings, textiles, clothing, jewellery, and much more. The atmosphere is lively and bustling, a true feast for the senses. Be prepared to browse extensively and haggle for prices. It's not just for tourists; many Lusaka residents frequent the market, making it a great place for people-watching and experiencing a slice of local weekend life.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at Arcades Shopping Mall on Great East Road. Easily accessible by taxi.

Opening Hours: Sundays only, from roughly 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

Cost: Free to browse.

Website: N/A

Kalimba Reptile Park

A fantastic outing for families and anyone interested in the slithery and scaly side of the animal kingdom. Kalimba Reptile Park is home to an impressive collection of crocodiles, snakes, tortoises, and other reptiles. Guided tours provide fascinating information about the different species, including some of Zambia's most venomous snakes, safely housed behind glass. The highlight for many is the Nile crocodile enclosure, where you can see these prehistoric giants basking in the sun. The park also has a pleasant recreational area with swimming pools, a playground, and a restaurant famous for its crocodile meat dishes (for the adventurous foodie). It’s a well-maintained and educational facility that offers a different kind of wildlife experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the Ngwerere area, about a 20-30 minute drive north-east of the city center.

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily.

Cost: Around 100-150 ZMW for adults, with discounts for children.

Website: http://www.kalimba-farms.com/

Lusaka National Museum

To understand the soul of Zambia, a visit to the Lusaka National Museum is essential. The museum is split into two main galleries. The lower gallery is dedicated to the country's rich history and ethnography, showcasing traditional village life, witchcraft artifacts, and the long struggle for independence from British rule. The upper gallery features contemporary Zambian art, displaying a rotating collection of paintings and sculptures from talented local artists. While not as large as some international museums, it offers a poignant and comprehensive look into the nation's past and its vibrant cultural present. The exhibits provide crucial context for the country you are visiting, making it a highly recommended first stop on your Lusaka itinerary.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Independence Avenue, close to the city center and government buildings.

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 4:30 PM daily.

Cost: Approx. $5 USD for international visitors.

Website: https://www.museums.org.zm/lusaka-national-museum/

Freedom Statue

This iconic monument is one of Lusaka's most important landmarks. Located on Independence Avenue, the Freedom Statue depicts a man breaking his chains, a powerful symbol of Zambia's liberation from colonial rule. The statue commemorates the freedom fighters who died during the struggle for independence, which was achieved in 1964. It’s a place of national pride and is often the site of official commemorations. While you only need a short time to see it, a visit offers a moment of reflection on the country's history. It's an excellent photo opportunity and is located close to the Lusaka National Museum, making it easy to combine the two sights.

Practical Information

How to Get There: On Independence Avenue, in the heart of the government district. Easily visible and accessible.

Opening Hours: 24/7 (public monument).

Cost: Free.

Website: N/A

37d Gallery

Formerly known as the Henry Tayali Gallery, the 37d Gallery is the premier venue for contemporary art in Zambia. Located in the leafy suburbs of Kabulonga, the gallery showcases the work of both established and emerging Zambian artists through a series of thoughtfully curated exhibitions. It provides a platform for modern creative expression, covering painting, sculpture, photography, and mixed media. The gallery is part of the broader international art scene, often participating in art fairs and promoting Zambian talent abroad. For art lovers, it is an unmissable stop to see the pulse of Zambia's modern art scene and perhaps purchase a unique piece of work.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Leopards Lane, off Leopards Hill Road in Kabulonga. Best reached by taxi.

Opening Hours: Tuesday - Friday: 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM, Saturday: 10:00 AM - 1:00 PM. Closed Sunday & Monday.

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: https://www.37dgallery.com/

Soweto Market

For a truly immersive, chaotic, and authentic local experience, dive into Soweto Market. This is not a tourist market; it's one of Lusaka's largest and most hectic hubs of commerce. Here, you'll find everything from fresh produce and dried fish to secondhand clothes ('salaula'), electronics, and household goods. The market is a sensory overload in the best way possible—a maze of narrow alleys, shouting vendors, and a kaleidoscope of colours and smells. It offers a raw and unfiltered glimpse into the daily life of Lusaka's residents. It's advisable to go with a local guide or be very mindful of your belongings, but for the adventurous traveler, it’s an unforgettable experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located west of the city center's main bus station (Kulima Tower). A short taxi ride is best.

Opening Hours: Daily, from early morning until late afternoon.

Cost: Free to browse.

Website: N/A

Munda Wanga Environmental Park

Established in the 1950s, Munda Wanga is one of Zambia's oldest conservation and educational centers. The park is split into two sections: a botanical garden and a wildlife sanctuary. The botanical gardens showcase a diverse range of native and exotic plants, offering a peaceful retreat from the city. The wildlife sanctuary is home to a variety of rescued animals that cannot be returned to the wild, including lions, tigers, primates, and various bird species. While it has faced challenges with upkeep over the years, it remains a beloved local spot for picnics and family outings, and a visit supports its ongoing environmental education programs.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located south of Lusaka, off Kafue Road. Best reached by car or taxi.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily.

Cost: Approx. 80-100 ZMW for adults.

Website: https://www.mundawanga.com/

Chaminuka Game Reserve

For a more exclusive, all-inclusive safari experience without travelling far from Lusaka, Chaminuka Game Reserve is an excellent choice. This private reserve, about an hour's drive from the city, offers a luxurious day trip or overnight stay. Day packages typically include a game drive, a boat cruise on the lake, a guided bush walk, and a gourmet lunch. The reserve is home to over 70 species of wildlife, including giraffe, zebra, puku, and even lions and cheetahs in separate enclosures. It's a curated safari experience that combines wildlife viewing with high-end hospitality and a vast collection of African art displayed throughout the main lodge.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located east of Kenneth Kaunda International Airport. The reserve can arrange transfers from Lusaka.

Opening Hours: Day trips and overnight stays must be pre-booked.

Cost: Day trips start from around $150 USD per person, including activities and lunch.

Website: https://chaminuka.com/

St. Ignatius Catholic Church

An unexpected architectural gem in Lusaka, St. Ignatius Church is a striking example of modern, African-inspired ecclesiastical design. Built in the 1960s, its most prominent feature is the massive, tent-like copper roof, which pays homage to Zambia's primary natural resource. Inside, the church is spacious and filled with natural light, featuring beautiful stained-glass windows and locally crafted wooden furnishings. It's an active place of worship but visitors are welcome to respectfully look around outside of service times. It represents a fascinating blend of faith, local materials, and contemporary design.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the Rhodes Park suburb, easily accessible by taxi.

Opening Hours: Open daily; best to visit outside of mass times.

Cost: Free.

Website: N/A

EastPark Mall

To see the modern face of Lusaka, spend some time at EastPark Mall. This large, open-air shopping center is a popular hub for dining, shopping, and entertainment among Lusaka's middle and upper classes. It features a wide range of international and South African chain stores, a modern cinema complex, a supermarket, and a fantastic selection of restaurants and cafes with outdoor seating. While not a traditional cultural attraction, visiting EastPark provides valuable insight into the contemporary lifestyle, aspirations, and consumer culture of urban Zambia. It’s a great place to have a meal, grab a coffee, and people-watch.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located opposite Arcades Shopping Mall on Great East Road.

Opening Hours: Shops typically 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM; restaurants open later.

Cost: Free to browse.

Website: https://eastparkmall.com/

Pakati Sunday Market

If you find the main Sunday Crafts Market at Arcades a bit too tourist-focused, head to the Pakati Market for a more local flavour. Located near the showgrounds, this market is also held on Sundays and features a mix of crafts, secondhand goods, plants, and food stalls. It's less polished and more eclectic, giving you a better sense of a genuine community market. You can find unique items and often at lower prices than at the more prominent tourist spots. It's an excellent alternative for those looking to get slightly off the beaten path and engage in a more authentic market experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located behind the Lusaka Showgrounds. A taxi is the easiest way to get there.

Opening Hours: Sundays only, roughly 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM.

Cost: Free to browse.

Website: N/A

Parays Game Ranch

Another excellent option for wildlife viewing near the city, Parays Game Ranch offers a relaxed and family-friendly environment. Located a short drive from Lusaka, the ranch is home to a variety of plains game such as giraffe, zebra, impala, and kudu. Visitors can take self-guided drives through the property or opt for guided tours. The main draw is the laid-back atmosphere, allowing you to spend a leisurely afternoon spotting animals. The ranch also features a restaurant and bar, making it a complete day-trip destination for both locals and tourists seeking a convenient dose of nature.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on the western side of Lusaka, off Mungwi Road. Requires a car or taxi.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM daily.

Cost: Entry fees apply, typically around 100-150 ZMW per person.

Website: N/A (Often found on local directory sites and Facebook).

Planning Your Visit to Lusaka

Getting Around

Getting around Lusaka is best done by taxi. Ride-sharing apps like Ulendo and Yango are popular, safe, and generally more affordable than hailing a cab on the street. For longer distances or day trips, hiring a private car and driver is a good option. Local minibuses are the cheapest way to travel but can be crowded and confusing for first-time visitors; they are recommended only for the more adventurous.

Where to Stay

The best areas to stay are the safe and pleasant suburbs of Kabulonga, Rhodes Park, and Longacres, which have numerous hotels, guesthouses, and restaurants. For luxury, the Radisson Blu Hotel Lusaka offers top-tier amenities. A great mid-range option is Lilayi Lodge, which provides a bush-lodge feel close to the city and the Elephant Nursery. For budget travelers, a reputable guesthouse like Lusaka Backpackers or a private room on Airbnb offers good value.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Lusaka is during the dry season, from May to October. The weather is mild and pleasant, with sunny days and cool nights, making it ideal for exploring the city and for wildlife viewing in the nearby parks. The 'emerald' or green season, from November to April, sees frequent rain and higher humidity, but the landscape is lush and beautiful, and it's the best time for birdwatching.

Suggested Itinerary

A simple 3-day itinerary: Day 1: History & Culture. Start at the Lusaka National Museum, then view the nearby Freedom Statue. In the afternoon, explore Kabwata Cultural Village for authentic crafts. Day 2: Wildlife Encounters. In the morning, visit the Lilayi Elephant Nursery for the 11:30 AM viewing. In the afternoon, go for a game drive in Lusaka National Park. Day 3: Markets & Modern Lusaka. If it's a Sunday, spend the morning at the Sunday Crafts Market at Arcades. If not, explore Soweto Market (for the adventurous) or Kabwata. In the afternoon, relax at a cafe or restaurant at EastPark Mall to experience modern Lusaka.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Lusaka expensive?
Lusaka can be moderately expensive, especially compared to rural Zambia, but it is generally more affordable than major Western cities. Accommodation will be your biggest expense, with international-style hotels costing $150-$300+ USD per night. Mid-range guesthouses are available for $50-$100. Eating at local restaurants is very cheap, while dining at upscale establishments in malls or hotels is comparable to Western prices. Activities like visiting national parks have tiered pricing, with international visitors paying the most, but costs are still reasonable (e.g., $15-$20 park entry).
How many days do you need in Lusaka?
Two to three full days are sufficient to cover the main highlights of Lusaka, including the key cultural sites, wildlife attractions on the outskirts, and markets. This allows for a relaxed pace. If you plan to use Lusaka as a base for longer day trips, like to Chaminuka Game Reserve, or if you want to explore the city's food and art scene more deeply, you could easily spend four to five days.
Is Lusaka safe?
Lusaka is relatively safe for tourists, but like any large city, it requires common sense and awareness. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag snatching, can occur, especially in crowded areas like markets and bus stations. It is advisable not to walk alone at night; use a reputable taxi service like Ulendo. Avoid displaying expensive valuables openly. The main tourist areas and affluent suburbs are generally secure, but be cautious in high-density residential areas ('compounds') and downtown after dark.
What is Lusaka famous for?
Lusaka is famous for being the political and commercial capital of Zambia. It's known as a gateway city for travelers heading to iconic destinations like Victoria Falls, South Luangwa, and Kafue National Parks. Locally, it is famous for its vibrant markets, from the massive Soweto Market to the popular Sunday Crafts Market. It is also increasingly recognized for its unique blend of urban life and accessible nature, with a national park and an elephant nursery right on its doorstep, offering wildlife experiences unmatched by most other capital cities.
