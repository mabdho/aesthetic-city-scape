15 Best Things to Do in Ahmedabad, India (2025 Guide)

Welcome to Ahmedabad, a captivating metropolis where centuries of history seamlessly blend with bustling modernity. As India's first UNESCO World Heritage City, Ahmedabad offers a rich tapestry of Indo-Islamic architecture, intricate Jain temples, and the living legacy of Mahatma Gandhi. Known as the 'Manchester of India' for its thriving textile industry, the city is a treasure trove for culture vultures, history buffs, and food lovers alike. From wandering through the ancient 'pols' (gated neighborhoods) to marveling at contemporary design, exploring this Gujarati gem is an unforgettable experience. Whether you're seeking spiritual solace at a serene ashram or indulging in the vibrant street food scene, this guide will walk you through all the best things to do in Ahmedabad for an incredible journey.

Complete Guide to Ahmedabad Attractions

Sabarmati Ashram (Gandhi Ashram)

Situated on the serene banks of the Sabarmati River, this was Mahatma Gandhi's residence for over a decade and the nerve center of India's freedom struggle. It was from here that he launched the historic Dandi March in 1930. The ashram's tranquil atmosphere invites introspection and provides a profound look into the life and philosophy of the 'Father of the Nation'. The complex includes several cottages, including 'Hridaya Kunj' where Gandhi lived, a museum showcasing his life through photographs and letters, and an archive of his writings. Walking through the peaceful grounds, you can feel the weight of history and the power of simplicity that defined Gandhi's movement. It's an essential pilgrimage for anyone interested in Indian history and the principles of non-violence.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Ashram Road, easily accessible by auto-rickshaw or ride-sharing apps from anywhere in the city.

Opening Hours: 8:30 AM to 6:30 PM, daily.

Cost: Free entry.

Website: https://gandhiashramsabarmati.org/

Adalaj Stepwell (Adalaj ni Vav)

A magnificent example of Indo-Islamic architecture, the Adalaj Stepwell is a five-story deep subterranean structure built in 1498. More than just a water source, these stepwells were vital community centers, providing refuge from the scorching summer heat and a place for social gatherings. The vav is intricately carved from top to bottom with motifs of flowers, elephants, birds, and mythological scenes. Descending the steps, you'll notice a significant drop in temperature. The octagonal landings are architectural marvels, designed to allow light and air to permeate to the lowest levels. The fusion of Islamic floral patterns with Hindu and Jain symbolism makes it a unique masterpiece and a photographer's dream.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the village of Adalaj, about 18 km north of Ahmedabad. Best reached by taxi or auto-rickshaw.

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, daily.

Jama Masjid

Built in 1424 by Sultan Ahmed Shah I, the founder of Ahmedabad, Jama Masjid is one of the most splendid mosques in India. Located in the heart of the old city, this grand structure was built using yellow sandstone and showcases a beautiful blend of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. Its vast courtyard is surrounded by an arcade of intricately carved pillars, creating a mesmerizing play of light and shadow. The main prayer hall features 260 columns supporting 15 domes of varying sizes, a design element borrowed from Hindu and Jain temple architecture. The serene ambiance inside offers a peaceful escape from the bustling city outside. Its central location makes it a key landmark on any tour of historic Ahmedabad.

Practical Information

How to Get There: In the Manek Chowk area of the old city. Easily reachable by auto-rickshaw.

Opening Hours: 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM. Closed to non-Muslims during prayer times.

Cost: Free entry. Modest dress is required.

Website: N/A

Sidi Saiyyed Mosque

Renowned worldwide for its exquisite stone latticework (jali), the Sidi Saiyyed Mosque is an icon of Ahmedabad's architectural heritage. Built in 1573, the last year of the Gujarat Sultanate, the mosque is famed for its ten intricately carved stone windows. The most celebrated jali is the 'Tree of Life', a stunning filigree of a tree with intertwining branches, which has become an unofficial symbol of the city of Ahmedabad. The mosque itself is a small, elegant structure, but it's the craftsmanship of the jalis that draws visitors from across the globe. The delicate artistry, carved from a single slab of stone, is best appreciated when sunlight filters through, casting beautiful patterns on the floor. It stands as a testament to the masterful skill of the artisans of that era.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at Lal Darwaja, in the city center. Accessible by all forms of local transport.

Opening Hours: 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM, daily.

Cost: Free entry.

Website: N/A

Calico Museum of Textiles

The Calico Museum is one of the world's foremost institutions dedicated to Indian textiles. Managed by the Sarabhai Foundation, it houses a spectacular collection of fabrics, tapestries, and costumes spanning five centuries. The exhibits include rare textiles, court carpets, tribal fabrics, and religious cloth, showcasing the incredible diversity and craftsmanship of Indian weavers and dyers. Due to the delicate nature of the exhibits, access is strictly controlled and limited. The museum is divided into two sections: 'Haveli' for religious textiles and 'Chauk' for courtly textiles. A visit requires advance online booking, often weeks or even months ahead, but the breathtaking collection makes it well worth the effort for anyone interested in art, design, or history.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the Shahibaug area. Best reached by taxi or auto-rickshaw.

Opening Hours: 10:30 AM to 12:30 PM. Closed on Wednesdays and public holidays. Guided tours only.

Cost: Free entry, but mandatory online registration is required well in advance.

Website: https://www.calicomuseum.org/

Hutheesing Jain Temple

Constructed in 1848, the Hutheesing Jain Temple is a stunning architectural marvel dedicated to Dharmanatha, the 15th Jain Tirthankara. Built entirely of white marble, the temple is an oasis of peace and intricate beauty. The main edifice is surrounded by a courtyard with 52 smaller shrines, each dedicated to a different Tirthankara, creating a magnificent complex. The detailed carvings, domed ceilings, and elegant pillars showcase the exceptional skill of the Salvi community of artisans. The Manastambha (column of honour), inspired by the one in Chittorgarh, stands majestically at the entrance. It's a place of both religious significance and artistic appreciation.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Bardolpura, outside the Delhi Darwaja.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, daily.

Cost: Free entry. Photography may be restricted inside the main shrine.

Website: N/A

Manek Chowk

Manek Chowk is the vibrant, beating heart of Ahmedabad's old city, a place that transforms dramatically throughout the day. By morning, it's a bustling vegetable market. By afternoon, it functions as a bullion market. But as night falls, it undergoes its most famous transformation, becoming a legendary open-air street food paradise. Tables and chairs fill the square as vendors set up stalls selling an incredible array of Gujarati delicacies. From buttery pav bhaji and crispy dosas to unique creations like the chocolate sandwich and Gwalior dosa, the options are endless. The atmosphere is electric, filled with the sizzle of hot pans and the chatter of locals and tourists alike. A visit here is a quintessential Ahmedabad experience.

Kankaria Lake

Originally built by Sultan Qutb-ud-Din in the 15th century, Kankaria Lake is the largest lake in Ahmedabad and has been developed into a vibrant recreational hub. The circular lakefront is perfect for jogging, walking, or simply relaxing. In the center of the lake is an island garden called Nagina Wadi, connected by a walkway. The complex surrounding the lake offers a plethora of attractions, including a zoo, an amusement park (Balvatika), a toy train, water rides, and hot air balloon rides. In the evening, the lakefront comes alive with a spectacular multi-colored light show. It's a favorite spot for families and a great place to spend a leisurely afternoon or evening.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the Maninagar area. Easily accessible by BRTS bus, auto-rickshaw, or taxi.

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM to 10:00 PM. Closed on Mondays.

Cost: Nominal entry fee for the lakefront (around â‚¹25). Additional charges for attractions.

Auto World Vintage Car Museum

A treat for automobile enthusiasts, the Auto World Vintage Car Museum showcases a remarkable collection of classic and antique vehicles. The museum is the personal collection of the Pranlal Bhogilal family and features over 100 cars from brands like Rolls-Royce, Bentley, Maybach, Cadillac, and Mercedes. Beyond just cars, the collection includes vintage motorcycles, horse-drawn carriages, and other utility vehicles. Each vehicle is meticulously maintained and tells a story of its era. For an extra fee, you can even take a spin in one of these magnificent vintage cars for a truly memorable experience.

Sarkhej Roza

Often referred to as 'Ahmedabad's Acropolis' by the famous architect Le Corbusier, Sarkhej Roza is a stunning mosque and tomb complex. Its architecture is notable for its complete absence of arches, relying instead on piers and beams, showcasing a unique pre-Mughal Islamic style deeply influenced by local traditions. The complex is centered around a large artificial tank. The serene and elegant design creates a meditative atmosphere. It contains the tombs of the Sufi saint Shaikh Ahmed Khattu Ganj Bakhsh, Sultan Mahmud Begada, and his queen. The intricate jali work, spacious courtyards, and minimalist aesthetic make it a peaceful retreat and a masterpiece of regional architecture.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the suburb of Sarkhej, about 8 km from the city center. Accessible by auto-rickshaw or taxi.

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, daily.

Cost: Free entry.

Website: N/A

Gujarat Science City

Gujarat Science City is an ambitious initiative by the state government to foster scientific temperament among the public. It's a sprawling complex filled with interactive exhibits, an IMAX 3D theatre, a planetarium, and an energy education park. The Hall of Space and Hall of Science offer hands-on exhibits that make learning fun for all ages. One of its main highlights is the state-of-the-art aquatic gallery, one of the largest in India, featuring a variety of marine species in stunningly designed tanks, including a walk-through tunnel aquarium. It's an excellent destination for families and anyone with a curious mind, offering a full day of educational entertainment.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located off SG Highway. Best reached by taxi or auto-rickshaw.

Opening Hours: 10:00 AM to 7:30 PM, daily.

Law Garden Night Market

As dusk settles, the area around Law Garden transforms into a bustling open-air market, a paradise for shoppers looking for traditional Gujarati handicrafts. The market is famous for its vibrant textiles, including bandhani (tie-dye) and block-printed fabrics, embroidered wall hangings, and intricate mirror-work dresses (chaniya cholis). You can also find a wide variety of jewelry, accessories, and decorative items. The atmosphere is lively and colorful, and bargaining is part of the experience. It's the perfect place to pick up authentic souvenirs and witness local craftsmanship firsthand. After shopping, you can enjoy street food from the numerous stalls nearby.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at Law Garden, Netaji Road, in the Ellisbridge area.

Opening Hours: From evening (around 7:00 PM) until late night.

Cost: Free to browse. Prices are negotiable.

Website: N/A

Bhadra Fort & Teen Darwaza

Bhadra Fort, built by Sultan Ahmed Shah in 1411, is the historical foundation of Ahmedabad. This fortified complex once housed royal palaces, mosques, and a vast open courtyard. Today, the revitalized Bhadra Plaza hosts cultural events and markets. Within the fort is the Bhadrakali Temple, which is highly revered by locals. Just east of the fort is Teen Darwaza (Three Gates), the historic ceremonial entrance to the Royal Square. This imposing gateway is another architectural icon of the city. An eternal lamp has been burning in one of its alcoves for over 600 years in honor of the goddess Lakshmi. Exploring this area gives you a feel for the city's royal past.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the heart of the old city, near Manek Chowk and Jama Masjid.

Opening Hours: Fort and surrounding areas are generally accessible throughout the day.

Cost: Free entry.

Website: N/A

Vikram Sarabhai Space Exhibition

Dedicated to the father of India's space program, Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, this exhibition is a fascinating stop for science and technology enthusiasts. Run by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), it showcases the history and achievements of India's space missions. The exhibition features interactive models, life-size satellites, and rocket prototypes. Visitors can learn about the workings of satellites, launch vehicles like the PSLV and GSLV, and the applications of space technology in everyday life. It's a compact but highly informative museum that offers a glimpse into India's impressive journey into space, inspiring for both children and adults.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the heart of the city at the Ahmedabad Management Association (AMA) complex.

Opening Hours: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Closed on Mondays.

Cost: Free entry.

Website: https://www.isro.gov.in/VSSE_Ahemedabad.html

Planning Your Visit to Ahmedabad

Getting Around

Ahmedabad has a well-connected public transport system. Auto-rickshaws are ubiquitous and convenient for short distances; always agree on a fare or use the meter. Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Ola are widely available and offer competitive pricing. For longer routes, the Bus Rapid Transit System (BRTS) is efficient and covers large parts of the city. The Ahmedabad Metro is a newer, clean, and fast option for specific corridors.

Where to Stay

For first-time visitors, staying near CG Road or Ashram Road provides central access to attractions and restaurants. Navrangpura is a slightly more upscale area with many hotels and cafes. For a luxury experience, consider hotels along the Sabarmati Riverfront for beautiful views. Budget travelers can find good options in the Paldi area. Recommended hotels include the Hyatt Regency (Luxury), Fortune Landmark (Mid-Range), and The House of MG (Heritage).

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Ahmedabad is during the cooler, drier months from October to March. The weather is pleasant, with daytime temperatures ranging from 20Â°C to 30Â°C, perfect for sightseeing. The summers (April to June) are extremely hot and dry, while the monsoon season (July to September) brings heavy rainfall, which can disrupt travel plans. Visiting during festivals like Navratri (October) or Uttarayan (International Kite Festival, January 14th) offers a unique cultural experience.

Suggested Itinerary

A Simple 3-Day Itinerary: Day 1: Historic Heart - Start with the morning Heritage Walk. Explore Jama Masjid, Sidi Saiyyed Mosque, and Bhadra Fort. In the evening, dive into the culinary scene at Manek Chowk. Day 2: Gandhi & Grandeur - Visit the serene Sabarmati Ashram in the morning. In the afternoon, travel to the magnificent Adalaj Stepwell and then the elegant Sarkhej Roza. Day 3: Culture & Leisure - Book a morning slot for the Calico Museum of Textiles (book well in advance). Spend the afternoon at Kankaria Lakefront. End your trip with souvenir shopping at the Law Garden Night Market.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ahmedabad expensive?
Ahmedabad is one of the more affordable major cities in India. Accommodation can be found across all budget ranges. Street food and local restaurants are very inexpensive, allowing you to eat well for under â‚¹500 a day. Transportation via auto-rickshaws and BRTS is also very cost-effective. While high-end hotels and restaurants exist, a comfortable trip can be managed on a modest budget.
How many days do you need in Ahmedabad?
To cover the main highlights of Ahmedabad, a well-planned 3-day trip is sufficient. This allows you to explore the UNESCO World Heritage old city, visit the Sabarmati Ashram, and see key architectural sites like the Adalaj Stepwell. If you wish to explore at a more relaxed pace, visit more museums, or take a day trip to nearby sites like Modhera Sun Temple, extending your stay to 4 or 5 days is recommended.
Is Ahmedabad safe?
Ahmedabad is generally considered a very safe city for tourists, including solo female travelers. Like any large city, it's wise to take standard precautions: be aware of your surroundings, avoid poorly lit areas at night, and keep your valuables secure. It's important to note that Gujarat is a 'dry state,' meaning the sale and consumption of alcohol is restricted and requires a special permit for tourists, which can be obtained at select hotels.
What is Ahmedabad famous for?
Ahmedabad is famous for being India's first UNESCO World Heritage City, recognized for its rich architectural heritage from the Sultanate period. It is renowned for its connection to Mahatma Gandhi, with the Sabarmati Ashram being a key landmark. The city is also a textile hub, known for its Calico Museum and vibrant markets. Architecturally, it's famous for intricate stepwells (vavs) and stunning mosques with unique 'jali' work, like the Sidi Saiyyed Mosque. Lastly, its incredible vegetarian street food, especially at Manek Chowk, is legendary.
