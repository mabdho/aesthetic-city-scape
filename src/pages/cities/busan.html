15 Best Things to Do in Busan, South Korea (2025 Guide)

Welcome to Busan, South Korea's vibrant coastal gem where the mountains meet the sea. This bustling port city offers a captivating blend of stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and modern urban energy. From sprawling sandy beaches and ancient temples perched on cliffs to the world's largest fish market, Busan is a feast for the senses. You can wander through colorful, art-filled villages, indulge in some of the freshest seafood imaginable, or hike along breathtaking coastal trails. Whether you're a beach lover, a foodie, a culture enthusiast, or an adventure seeker, you'll find an incredible array of experiences. Get ready to explore this dynamic city and discover the very best things to do in Busan.

Complete Guide to Busan Attractions

Gamcheon Culture Village

Often called the 'Machu Picchu of Busan,' Gamcheon Culture Village is a kaleidoscope of color cascading down a coastal hillside. This former slum was transformed into a vibrant cultural hub by residents and artists, who painted the houses in brilliant hues and installed unique art pieces and sculptures throughout its labyrinthine alleys. Visitors can follow a stamp trail map, available at the entrance, to discover hidden galleries, quaint cafes, and stunning photo opportunities, including the famous 'Little Prince and Fennec Fox' statue overlooking the village. Exploring Gamcheon is an adventure in itself. As you navigate the steep stairways and narrow paths, you'll uncover charming workshops and shops selling local crafts. The village is a living testament to the power of art and community in urban regeneration, offering not just picturesque views but a deep sense of local history and creativity. It's an essential stop for anyone wanting to experience Busan's artistic soul.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Toseong Station (Line 1), Exit 6. From there, take local bus Saha 1-1, Seogu 2, or Seogu 2-2 to the Gamcheon Culture Village Entrance.

Opening Hours: Village is open 24/7, but shops and galleries typically operate from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Cost: Free to enter. A stamp trail map costs around 2,000 KRW.

Website: www.gamcheon.or.kr

Haeundae Beach

Haeundae is arguably South Korea's most famous beach, a 1.5-kilometer stretch of pristine white sand that draws crowds year-round. In summer, it's a bustling hotspot for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports, with a lively atmosphere that lasts well into the night. The beachfront is lined with luxury hotels, trendy restaurants, and chic bars, making it the heart of modern Busan. Beyond the summer fun, Haeundae hosts various festivals, including the Sand Festival in spring and the Polar Bear Swim in winter. A stroll along the beach leads to Dongbaekseom Island, a beautiful park with a coastal walking path and the Nurimaru APEC House. Whether you're looking for relaxation, entertainment, or a scenic walk, Haeundae Beach is a quintessential Busan experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Haeundae Station (Line 2), Exits 3 or 5, and walk straight for about 500 meters.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7. Swimming hours are typically 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM during the summer season.

Cost: Free.

Website: sunnfun.haeundae.go.kr

Jagalchi Fish Market

As Korea's largest and most famous seafood market, Jagalchi is a thrilling assault on the senses. The market's slogan, 'Oiso, boiso, saiso!' ('Come, see, and buy!'), perfectly captures the vibrant energy of this bustling hub. The ground floor is a sprawling wet market where you can see an incredible variety of live and fresh seafood, from giant crabs and wriggling octopuses to countless species of fish. Most vendors are 'Jagalchi Ajummas,' the powerful middle-aged or older women who run the stalls. The real highlight is picking your own seafood downstairs and having it prepared for you at one of the restaurants on the second floor. Choose from sashimi (hoe), grilled fish (gui), or a spicy seafood stew (maeuntang) for an unbelievably fresh and authentic meal. The outdoor market section and the rooftop terrace, which offers panoramic views of the port, are also worth exploring.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Jagalchi Station (Line 1), Exit 10. Walk for about 5 minutes towards the harbor.

Opening Hours: Market: 5:00 AM to 10:00 PM. Restaurants may have different hours. Closed on the first and third Tuesday of every month.

Cost: Free to enter. Meal prices vary depending on your selection.

Website: jagalchimarket.bisco.or.kr

Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

While most Korean temples are nestled deep in the mountains, Haedong Yonggungsa is dramatically situated on the rocky coastline of northeastern Busan, making it one of the most unique and beautiful temples in the country. Founded in 1376, its motto is 'At least one of your wishes will be answered here through your heartfelt prayers.' The temple's stunning seaside location offers breathtaking views, especially at sunrise, when the light catches the intricate details of the pagodas and statues. Visitors enter through a tunnel-like passage flanked by the 12 zodiac animal statues before crossing a bridge and descending 108 steps, symbolizing the 108 agonies of Buddhism. The complex includes a three-story pagoda, several golden Buddha statues, and the main sanctuary, all harmoniously integrated with the rugged coastal landscape. The sound of the waves crashing against the rocks adds a powerful, meditative quality to the experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: From Haeundae Station (Line 2), Exit 7, take Bus 181 and get off at the Yonggungsa Temple bus stop.

Opening Hours: 5:00 AM to sunset, daily.

Cost: Free.

Website: www.yongkungsa.or.kr

Taejongdae Resort Park

Located at the southern tip of Yeongdo Island, Taejongdae Resort Park is a natural wonderland of dense evergreen forests, magnificent sea cliffs, and endless ocean vistas. The park is named after King Taejong Muyeol of the Silla Dynasty, who was captivated by its beauty. The highlight is the Yeongdo Lighthouse, perched on a sheer cliff 250 meters above the sea. From the observatory, you can enjoy stunning panoramic views, and on a clear day, you might even spot Japan's Tsushima Island. To explore the park, you can hike the scenic trails or take the convenient 'Danubi' train, which makes stops at key viewpoints, including the observatory and the Taejongsa Temple. The rugged rock formations along the coast, shaped by centuries of wind and waves, are a key attraction. For a different perspective, consider taking a cruise boat around the cliffs.

Practical Information

How to Get There: From Busan Station, take Bus 88 or 101 and get off at the Taejongdae terminus.

Opening Hours: March-Oct: 5:00 AM - 12:00 AM; Nov-Feb: 6:00 AM - 12:00 AM. Danubi Train: 9:20 AM - 5:30 PM.

Cost: Park entry is free. Danubi Train: Adults 3,000 KRW, Teenagers 2,000 KRW, Children 1,500 KRW.

Website: www.bisco.or.kr/taejongdae

Gwangalli Beach

Gwangalli Beach offers a more relaxed and youthful vibe compared to Haeundae, but its main draw is the spectacular view of the Gwangandaegyo Bridge (Diamond Bridge). This wide, crescent-shaped beach is famous for its fine sand and is lined with a fantastic selection of cafes, pubs, and seafood restaurants. It's a popular spot for both locals and tourists to unwind and socialize. The real magic happens at night when the Gwangandaegyo Bridge lights up in a dazzling, ever-changing display of colors. The reflection on the water creates a mesmerizing scene. Many visitors gather on the sand or at a beachfront bar to watch the light show. Gwangalli also hosts drone light shows on Saturday evenings, adding another layer of spectacle to this iconic Busan view.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Gwangan Station (Line 2), Exits 3 or 5, and walk for about 10 minutes.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7.

Cost: Free.

Website: gwangalli.suyeong.go.kr

BIFF Square (Busan International Film Festival Square)

Located in the bustling Nampo-dong district, BIFF Square is the heart of Busan's film culture. This street was once the home of the Busan International Film Festival (BIFF), Asia's most prominent film festival. While the main festival events have moved to the Busan Cinema Center, the square remains a vibrant tribute to cinema. Look down, and you'll find handprints of famous film directors and actors embedded in the pavement, similar to Hollywood's Walk of Fame. The area is also a street food paradise. It's particularly famous for Ssiat Hotteok, a sweet Korean pancake filled with a mixture of seeds and brown sugar syrup. The lively atmosphere, surrounded by movie theaters, shops, and food stalls, makes it a must-visit destination for entertainment and delicious local snacks.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Jagalchi Station (Line 1), Exit 7.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7, though shops and stalls have individual hours (typically late morning until late evening).

Cost: Free to walk around. Street food is very affordable.

Website: N/A

Seomyeon

Seomyeon is Busan's central commercial, entertainment, and nightlife district. It's the bustling heart of the city, where two subway lines intersect, making it a major transportation hub. The area is packed with department stores like Lotte and NC, underground shopping centers, countless fashion boutiques, and cosmetic shops. By day, it's a shopper's paradise. As evening falls, Seomyeon transforms into the city's premier nightlife destination. The streets come alive with neon lights, and the area's numerous restaurants, pubs, and clubs fill up with young people. Seomyeon Medical Street is also famous for its high concentration of cosmetic and medical clinics. Whether you're here to shop, eat, or party, Seomyeon offers a dynamic slice of modern Busan life.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Seomyeon Station (interchange for Lines 1 and 2).

Opening Hours: Shops are generally open 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM. Bars and clubs stay open late.

Cost: Free to explore.

Website: N/A

Beomeosa Temple

Nestled on the slopes of Geumjeongsan Mountain, Beomeosa Temple is one of Korea's most important spiritual sites and a head temple of the Jogye Order of Korean Buddhism. Originally constructed over 1,300 years ago, it has been rebuilt several times after being destroyed during invasions. The temple's architecture is a stunning example of Joseon Dynasty design, harmoniously blending with the surrounding forest. Visitors can admire the main hall, Daeungjeon, and the three-story stone pagoda, both designated as national treasures. The temple complex is serene and expansive, offering a peaceful retreat from the city's hustle. For active visitors, Beomeosa serves as a popular starting point for hiking up Geumjeongsan Mountain to see the Geumjeongsanseong Fortress walls. The temple also offers temple stay programs for those wishing to experience monastic life.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Beomeosa Station (Line 1), Exits 5 or 7. From there, take Bus 90 to the temple entrance.

Opening Hours: 8:30 AM - 5:30 PM, daily.

Cost: Free.

Website: www.beomeo.kr

Songdo Cloud Trails & Cable Car

Songdo Beach, Korea's first official public beach, has been revitalized with stunning modern attractions. The centerpiece is the Songdo Cloud Trails (Skywalk), a 365-meter-long curving walkway that extends out over the sea. Sections of the walkway feature a glass floor, allowing you to see the ocean right beneath your feet, offering a thrilling and picturesque experience. For an even more spectacular view, take the Busan Air Cruise (cable car) which travels from Songnim Park on the east side of the beach to Amnam Park on the west. You can choose between a standard cabin or a 'crystal cruise' cabin with a transparent floor for a bird's-eye view of the beach, skywalk, and the coastline. Amnam Park itself offers beautiful walking trails through the forest.

Practical Information

How to Get There: From Jagalchi Station (Line 1), Exit 2, take Bus 7, 26, 71, or 96 and get off at Songdo Beach.

Opening Hours: Cloud Trails: 6:00 AM - 11:00 PM. Cable Car: 9:00 AM - 8:00 PM (hours vary by season).

Cost: Cloud Trails are free. Cable Car round trip: approx. 15,000 KRW (standard) to 20,000 KRW (crystal).

Website: www.busanaircruise.co.kr

Oryukdo Skywalk

Jutting out from a coastal cliff, the Oryukdo Skywalk is a U-shaped glass bridge that offers the thrilling sensation of walking on air above the sea. The name 'Oryukdo' refers to the group of five or six (depending on the tide) rocky islets just off the coast, which the skywalk provides a perfect view of. The floor is made of thick, transparent glass, giving you a dizzying but spectacular view of the waves crashing onto the cliffs below. Located at the starting point of the Igidae Coastal Walk, the skywalk is a quick but memorable stop. The combination of the clear blue water, the rugged islands, and the sheer cliffs makes for incredible photos. It's a testament to Busan's ability to blend its stunning natural landscapes with innovative modern design.

Practical Information

How to Get There: From Busan Station, take Bus 27 and get off at the Oryukdo SK View back gate stop (the last stop).

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM (may be closed during bad weather).

Cost: Free.

Website: www.bsnamgu.go.kr/eng/main.asp

Gukje Market

One of Korea's largest traditional markets, Gukje Market is a sprawling, chaotic, and fascinating place to explore. Its name means 'International Market,' a title earned after the Korean War when refugees began selling goods supplied by US troops. Today, it's a labyrinth of alleys packed with stalls selling just about everything imaginable, from electronics and kitchenware to clothing and souvenirs. The market is directly connected to other famous markets like Bupyeong Kkangtong Market (known for its night market) and Jagalchi Market, creating a massive shopping district. A key section is 'Eating Street,' where you can sit on small stools and sample classic Korean street foods like tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), bibim dangmyeon (spicy glass noodles), and gimbap. Getting lost in Gukje Market is part of the fun and offers a true slice of local life.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Jagalchi Station (Line 1), Exit 7. The market is across the street from BIFF Square.

Opening Hours: Approximately 9:00 AM - 8:00 PM. Many stalls closed on the 1st and 3rd Sunday of the month.

Cost: Free to enter.

Website: gukjemarket.co.kr

Busan Tower & Yongdusan Park

Rising from the center of Yongdusan Park, the 120-meter-tall Busan Tower is an iconic symbol of the city. While not as tall as modern skyscrapers, its location atop a hill in the city center provides fantastic 360-degree views of the port, the coastline, and the surrounding urban landscape. The observation deck is particularly beautiful at night when the city lights sparkle below. Yongdusan Park itself, named for its resemblance to a dragon's head ('yongdu' means dragon's head), is a peaceful oasis in the heart of the bustling Nampo-dong area. The park features a statue of the great naval hero Admiral Yi Sun-sin, a flower clock, and numerous benches where locals and tourists relax. It's a great place to take a break from shopping and enjoy some green space and panoramic views.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Take the subway to Nampo Station (Line 1), Exit 1, and use the escalator to reach the park.

Opening Hours: Park is open 24/7. Tower Observatory: 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM.

Cost: Park entry is free. Tower admission is approx. 12,000 KRW for adults.

Website: www.diamondtower.co.kr

Shinsegae Centum City

Officially certified by the Guinness World Records as the world's largest department store, Shinsegae Centum City is more than just a shopping mall; it's a massive entertainment complex. You can find everything here, from luxury designer brands and international chains to a huge bookstore and a gourmet food market. It's a sleek, modern, and overwhelming destination for retail therapy. Beyond shopping, Shinsegae houses a multiplex cinema, an ice rink, and a large indoor golf range. However, its most famous feature is 'Spa Land,' a luxurious and modern take on the traditional Korean jjimjilbang (bathhouse). Spa Land offers 22 different spa baths fed by natural hot spring water, 13 distinctively themed saunas and jjimjilbangs, and an open-air foot spa. It's the perfect place to relax and rejuvenate after a long day of sightseeing.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Directly connected to Centum City Station (Subway Line 2).

Opening Hours: Department Store: 10:30 AM - 8:00 PM. Spa Land: 9:00 AM - 10:00 PM.

Cost: Free to enter the mall. Spa Land admission is approx. 23,000 KRW for 4 hours.

Website: www.shinsegae.com

Igidae Coastal Walk

For some of the most dramatic and rewarding views of Busan's skyline, the Igidae Coastal Walk is unbeatable. This 4.7-kilometer trail hugs the rugged coastline, offering a moderately challenging hike with stunning scenery. The path winds up and down the cliffs, through forests, and over wooden suspension bridges, providing constantly changing perspectives of the sea and the city. The trail's main appeal is its incredible view across the water towards Haeundae's skyscrapers and the Gwangandaegyo Bridge. The contrast between the raw, natural beauty of the park and the glittering urban landscape is breathtaking. The walk typically starts at the Oryukdo Skywalk and ends near Gwangalli, or vice-versa. It's an excellent way to experience Busan's natural environment without venturing too far from the city.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Start at the Oryukdo Skywalk (see directions above) or from the south end of Gwangalli Beach by taking a taxi to the Igidae entrance.

Opening Hours: Open 24/7, but best hiked during daylight hours for safety.

Cost: Free.

Website: N/A

Planning Your Visit to Busan

Getting Around

Busan has an excellent public transportation system. The subway is the most efficient way to travel between major districts, with four lines covering most tourist destinations. City buses are extensive and can get you closer to specific spots like temples. Taxis are plentiful and reasonably priced. For easy travel, purchase a T-money or Cashbee card, which can be used on subways, buses, and in taxis, and can be topped up at any convenience store or subway station.

Where to Stay

For first-time visitors, stay in Haeundae for beach access, modern hotels, and upscale dining (e.g., Paradise Hotel Busan, Shilla Stay Haeundae). For nightlife and shopping, base yourself in Seomyeon, the city's central hub (e.g., Lotte Hotel Busan, Arban Hotel). For a more traditional and cultural vibe with proximity to markets, choose Nampo-dong (e.g., GNB Hotel, Notte La Mia Hotel). Budget travelers can find excellent hostels and guesthouses in all these areas.

Best Time to Visit

The best times to visit Busan are during the spring (April-May) and autumn (September-November). During these seasons, the weather is mild and pleasant, perfect for sightseeing. Spring brings beautiful cherry blossoms, while autumn offers clear skies and colorful foliage. Summer (June-August) is hot, humid, and crowded, but it's the best time for beach activities. Winter (December-February) is milder than in Seoul and less crowded, offering a peaceful experience.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: East Busan Exploration. Start your morning at the stunning seaside Haedong Yonggungsa Temple. In the afternoon, relax or explore Haeundae Beach and Dongbaekseom Island. End your day with dinner and spectacular night views of the Diamond Bridge from Gwangalli Beach. Day 2: Culture & Markets. Spend the morning getting lost in the colorful alleys of Gamcheon Culture Village. Head to Nampo-dong for lunch at Jagalchi Fish Market, then explore Gukje Market and BIFF Square. In the evening, ascend Busan Tower in Yongdusan Park for night views. Day 3: Coastal Views & Relaxation. Start with the thrilling Oryukdo Skywalk, followed by a scenic hike along the Igidae Coastal Walk. Alternatively, visit Taejongdae Resort Park and take the Danubi train to the lighthouse. In the afternoon, relax at Songdo Beach and walk the Cloud Trails or pamper yourself at Spa Land in Shinsegae Centum City.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Busan expensive?
Busan is generally considered more affordable than Seoul. Public transportation is cheap and efficient. A wide range of dining options is available, from inexpensive street food and market stalls (under 10,000 KRW per meal) to mid-range restaurants and high-end dining. Accommodation costs vary widely, but budget-friendly guesthouses and mid-range hotels are plentiful. Many of the top attractions, like beaches and parks, are free.
How many days do you need in Busan?
A minimum of 3 full days is recommended to cover the main highlights without rushing. This allows you to dedicate a day to the eastern area (Haeundae, Gwangalli), a day to the central/western area (Gamcheon, Nampo-dong), and a day for nature parks like Taejongdae or Igidae. If you want to explore more deeply, visit more temples, or enjoy a more relaxed pace, 4 to 5 days would be ideal.
Is Busan safe?
Busan is a very safe city with a low crime rate, even at night. Standard travel precautions apply: be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas like markets and on public transport to avoid pickpocketing. It's safe for solo travelers, including women. The locals are generally friendly and helpful, though a translation app can be useful for communication.
What is Busan famous for?
Busan is famous for its unique blend of city and nature. Its key highlights include its beautiful beaches like Haeundae and Gwangalli, the enormous and lively Jagalchi Fish Market, the iconic Gamcheon Culture Village, and the stunning seaside Haedong Yonggungsa Temple. It is also renowned for hosting the prestigious Busan International Film Festival (BIFF) and for its delicious, fresh seafood and signature dishes like dwaeji gukbap (pork rice soup).
