15 Best Things to Do in Yogyakarta, Indonesia (2025 Guide)

Welcome to Yogyakarta, the undeniable soul of Java and the cultural heart of Indonesia. This vibrant city, often called 'Jogja', is a captivating blend of ancient traditions and youthful energy, where majestic temples stand minutes away from bustling art scenes and university life. It's a place where history is not just in museums but is lived daily in the halls of the Sultan's Palace and through the delicate art of batik making. From witnessing ethereal sunrises over volcanic peaks to exploring subterranean caves, Yogyakarta offers a journey for every type of traveler. Whether you are a history buff, an adventurer, or a culture enthusiast, you will find an incredible array of experiences. This guide will walk you through the very best things to do in Yogyakarta, ensuring your visit is nothing short of magical and unforgettable.

Top 5 Must-Visit Attractions

  • Borobudur Temple
  • Prambanan Temple
  • The Kraton (Sultan's Palace)
  • Mount Merapi
  • Taman Sari Water Castle

Complete Guide to Yogyakarta Attractions

Borobudur Temple

The world's largest Buddhist monument, Borobudur Temple, is an unmissable UNESCO World Heritage site. Dating back to the 9th century, this colossal temple consists of nine stacked platforms, six square and three circular, topped by a central dome. It is decorated with 2,672 relief panels and 504 Buddha statues. The sheer scale and intricate detail are breathtaking, depicting Buddhist cosmology and the journey to enlightenment. Visiting for sunrise is a truly spiritual experience. As the first light of day breaks through the morning mist, it illuminates the surrounding volcanoes and the temple's magnificent stupas, creating a serene and mystical atmosphere that will stay with you forever. It's a pilgrimage site and a masterpiece of human creative genius.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located 40 km northwest of Yogyakarta. Best accessed via a pre-booked tour, private driver, or by public bus from Jombor Terminal.

Opening Hours: 6:30 AM - 5:00 PM. Special sunrise tickets allow earlier access (approx. 4:30 AM). Note: Access to the temple structure itself is now limited and requires a special ticket.

Cost: Foreigners: ~IDR 455,000 for a regular ticket. Sunrise tours are more expensive. Combo tickets with Prambanan are available.

Website: https://borobudurpark.com/

Prambanan Temple

Another UNESCO World Heritage site, Prambanan is a stunning collection of massive Hindu temples dedicated to the Trimurti: Brahma (the Creator), Vishnu (the Preserver), and Shiva (the Destroyer). Built in the 9th century, this complex is characterized by its tall, pointed architecture, with the central Shiva temple soaring to an impressive 47 meters. The intricate carvings covering the temples tell the epic Hindu story of the Ramayana. Exploring the sprawling grounds and discovering the smaller surrounding temples is a delight. For a truly magical evening, catch the Ramayana Ballet performance with the illuminated temples as a dramatic backdrop.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located 17 km east of Yogyakarta. Accessible by TransJogja bus (Line 1A), taxi/ride-hailing, or private driver.

Opening Hours: 6:30 AM - 5:00 PM daily.

Cost: Foreigners: ~IDR 375,000. Combo tickets with Borobudur offer better value.

Website: https://prambanan.id/

The Kraton (Sultan's Palace)

The Kraton Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat is more than just a palace; it's the living, breathing heart of Javanese culture and the official residence of the Sultan of Yogyakarta. This sprawling complex is a city within a city, featuring ornate pavilions, spacious courtyards, and museums showcasing royal artifacts. It serves as a prime example of traditional Javanese palace architecture. Visitors can explore the main court and museum, watch cultural performances like gamelan music or classical Javanese dance, and get a glimpse into the enduring royal traditions. The palace guards, dressed in their traditional attire, add to the authentic and stately atmosphere of the place.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Centrally located in Yogyakarta, easily walkable from the Malioboro area or a short becak ride away.

Opening Hours: 8:30 AM - 2:00 PM (closes earlier on Fridays).

Cost: Approximately IDR 15,000 for foreigners, plus a small camera fee.

Website: https://www.kratonjogja.id/

Mount Merapi

For the adventurous, a trip to Mount Merapi, one of Indonesia's most active volcanoes, is a must. The most popular activity is the sunrise jeep tour. You'll ride in a classic Willis Jeep through rugged terrain, passing villages devastated by the last major eruption in 2010. The tour includes stops at a small museum with eruption artifacts and a 'bunker' with a tragic story. The highlight is reaching a viewpoint just before dawn to watch the sunrise over the volcanic landscape. The raw power and beauty of the volcano, often seen with smoke gently rising from its crater, make for an awe-inspiring and humbling experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Jeep tours start from Kaliurang, about an hour's drive north of Yogyakarta. Booking a tour package that includes hotel pickup is the easiest option.

Opening Hours: Tours typically start very early, around 4:00 AM for sunrise.

Cost: Jeep tours cost around IDR 450,000 - 600,000 per jeep (seats 3-4 people).

Website: N/A (Book through local tour operators)

Taman Sari Water Castle

Once a magnificent royal garden and pleasure palace for the Sultan and his harem, Taman Sari is a fascinating complex of pools, pavilions, and underground passages. Built in the mid-18th century, it served as a resting, bathing, and meditation area for the royal family. The main bathing complex, with its large pools and grand architecture, is the most photographed part. Beyond the pools, explore the surrounding maze-like alleyways of the Kampung Taman neighborhood, where you'll find the unique underground mosque (Sumur Gumuling). The entire area has a slightly decayed, mystical charm that transports you back in time.

Practical Information

How to Get There: A short walk southwest of the Kraton.

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM daily.

Cost: Approximately IDR 15,000 for foreigners.

Website: N/A

Jomblang Cave

Prepare for a truly otherworldly adventure at Jomblang Cave. This vertical cave is famous for the 'Light of Heaven,' a breathtaking phenomenon where a single beam of sunlight penetrates the darkness of the cave floor, illuminating the cavern in a divine glow. The experience begins with being lowered 60 meters into the sinkhole by a team of specialists using ropes and harnesses. After reaching the bottom, you'll trek through a muddy tunnel to reach the main chamber where the light shines through, typically between 10 AM and 12 PM. It's a challenging but incredibly rewarding journey that offers unparalleled photo opportunities and a sense of discovery.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Gunung Kidul, about a 90-minute to 2-hour drive from Yogyakarta. Booking a tour is essential as access is limited and requires specialized equipment.

Opening Hours: Only one descent per day, usually around 9:30 AM. You must arrive early. Pre-booking is mandatory.

Cost: Around IDR 450,000 - 500,000 per person, including equipment and lunch.

Website: N/A (Book through tour operators like Jomblang Cave Tour)

Timang Beach & Gondola

For thrill-seekers, Timang Beach offers an adrenaline-pumping experience unlike any other. This rugged coastline is known for its powerful waves crashing against dramatic cliffs. The main attraction is the traditional wooden gondola, manually operated by local villagers, which connects the mainland to the nearby Watu Panjang Islet across a churning sea. Alternatively, you can cross via a slightly more modern suspension bridge. Both options provide a heart-stopping ride and incredible views. The journey to the beach itself is part of the adventure, requiring a 4x4 jeep ride over rough, unpaved roads. It's a raw, untamed coastal experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Gunung Kidul, a 2-3 hour drive from Yogyakarta. You must hire a local jeep for the last few kilometers. Often combined with a Jomblang Cave tour.

Opening Hours: Daylight hours, typically 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM.

Cost: Gondola: ~IDR 200,000. Suspension Bridge: ~IDR 100,000. Jeep rental is separate.

Website: N/A

Malioboro Street

Malioboro is the pulsating main artery of Yogyakarta, a vibrant 24/7 street famous for shopping and street food. By day, it's packed with shops and stalls selling everything from batik shirts and leather goods to souvenirs and local snacks. By night, it transforms into a massive open-air food court, with countless 'lesehan' (mat-seating) food stalls setting up along the pavement. Strolling down Malioboro is an essential Jogja experience. Soak in the chaotic energy, haggle for a bargain, try local delicacies like 'gudeg' or 'kopi joss' (coffee with a hot charcoal), and watch the city come alive. It's the perfect place to feel the urban pulse of Yogyakarta.

Practical Information

How to Get There: The main street in the city center, easily accessible from anywhere.

Opening Hours: Shops are generally open 10:00 AM - 9:00 PM. Street food stalls open late into the night.

Cost: Free to walk. Shopping and food costs vary.

Website: N/A

Ramayana Ballet at Prambanan

Experience the epic Hindu tale of Ramayana brought to life through a spectacular performance of Javanese dance, drama, and music. Held in an open-air theater with the illuminated Prambanan temples as a magnificent backdrop, this ballet is a feast for the senses. Over 200 performers in vibrant costumes tell the story of King Rama and his quest to rescue his wife, Sita. The performance is a powerful display of Javanese artistic heritage. Even without understanding the language, the expressive dance, powerful gamelan orchestra, and dramatic setting make the story easy to follow and utterly captivating. It's an unforgettable cultural evening.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at the Prambanan Temple complex. Arrange a taxi for a round trip as the performance ends late.

Opening Hours: Performances typically start at 7:30 PM and run on select nights (check the schedule, especially during the dry season for open-air shows).

Cost: Ticket prices range from IDR 150,000 to IDR 450,000 depending on the seating class.

Website: https://borobudurpark.com/en/event/ramayana-prambanan/

Ullen Sentalu Museum

A world away from dusty, conventional museums, Ullen Sentalu is a private museum that offers a fascinating and intimate look into Javanese culture and the royal history of Yogyakarta and Solo. Located in the cool highlands of Kaliurang, the museum's architecture beautifully integrates with its natural surroundings. Visits are by guided tour only, which is a major highlight. The knowledgeable guides bring the artifacts to life, sharing stories of courtly life, love, and intrigue, focusing on the lives of royal princesses. The tour ends with a complimentary drink made from a traditional health recipe. It's a beautifully curated and highly engaging cultural experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Kaliurang, about 45-60 minutes north of Yogyakarta. Best reached by taxi or private car.

Opening Hours: 8:30 AM - 4:00 PM (Closed on Mondays).

Cost: Approximately IDR 100,000 for foreigners, including the mandatory guided tour.

Website: https://ullensentalu.com/

Kota Gede

Step into the historic heart of the old Mataram Sultanate in Kota Gede, a district renowned for its silver industry. This charming neighborhood is characterized by its narrow lanes, traditional 'Joglo' houses, and the lingering sense of a bygone era. The main draw is the multitude of silver workshops where you can watch artisans meticulously craft intricate jewelry and decorative items. Many workshops, like the famous HS Silver, offer tours and even allow you to try your hand at making your own piece of silver jewelry. Beyond the silver, explore the area to find the ancient Royal Cemetery and the old Great Mosque, soaking in the quiet, historic atmosphere.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located southeast of the city center. A short taxi or bus ride away.

Opening Hours: Workshops are generally open during business hours, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM.

Cost: Free to explore the area. Workshops are free to visit; classes have a fee.

Website: N/A

Pine Forest (Hutan Pinus Mangunan)

A haven for photographers and nature lovers, the Hutan Pinus Mangunan offers a tranquil escape from the city bustle. This vast man-made forest of towering, slender pine trees creates a magical and serene atmosphere. The play of light filtering through the canopy provides endless photo opportunities. Within the forest area, there are various viewpoints, art installations, and even a simple stage for performances. It's a popular spot for locals, especially on weekends. For the best light and fewest crowds, aim to visit on a weekday morning. The cool air and the scent of pine make it a refreshing and peaceful retreat.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the Mangunan area, about an hour's drive south of Yogyakarta. Best accessed by private car or scooter.

Opening Hours: 6:00 AM - 5:30 PM.

Cost: A small entrance fee of around IDR 10,000, plus parking fees.

Website: N/A

Plaosan Temple

Often overlooked in favor of its grand neighbor Prambanan, Plaosan Temple is a hidden gem that is well worth a visit. This 9th-century Buddhist temple complex is actually composed of two main temples, Plaosan Lor (North) and Plaosan Kidul (South). The temples are beautifully adorned with intricate carvings and statues of Bodhisattvas. What makes Plaosan unique is the blend of Buddhist and Hindu architectural elements, believed to symbolize the union of a Hindu prince and a Buddhist princess. The complex is surrounded by serene rice paddies, making it incredibly picturesque and far less crowded than the major temples. It offers a peaceful and intimate glimpse into Java's ancient past.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located just a few kilometers from Prambanan. You can easily visit it by bicycle or car after Prambanan.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM.

Cost: A small entrance fee of around IDR 10,000.

Website: N/A

Sonobudoyo Museum

Located just north of the Kraton, the Sonobudoyo Museum is the most comprehensive museum of Javanese history and culture in Yogyakarta. Housed in a beautiful traditional Javanese building, its collection includes an impressive array of artifacts, from puppets (wayang), masks, and batik to gamelan instruments, weapons (keris), and ancient statues. It provides excellent context for the cultural sights you'll see around the city. In the evenings, the museum hosts fantastic and affordable 'wayang kulit' (shadow puppet) performances, offering an authentic cultural experience in an intimate setting. It's a must-visit for anyone looking to deepen their understanding of Javanese heritage.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on the northern side of the main Alun-Alun (city square), a short walk from the Kraton or Malioboro.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM - 3:30 PM (Tues-Thurs, Sat, Sun), 8:00 AM - 11:30 AM (Fri). Closed on Mondays.

Cost: Entrance fee is around IDR 10,000. Wayang performance is ~IDR 20,000.

Website: http://www.sonobudoyo.com/

Alun-Alun Kidul (Southern Square)

For a uniquely Javanese evening, head to Alun-Alun Kidul, the southern square behind the Kraton. By day, it's a sleepy field with two large banyan trees at its center. By night, it transforms into a carnival-like atmosphere, filled with locals and tourists alike. The main attraction is renting a 'becak hias' or 'odong-odong' – wildly decorated, neon-lit pedal cars, often blasting loud music. Another famous activity is the 'masangin' challenge: attempting to walk blindfolded between the two central banyan trees. Legend says that those pure of heart will succeed, and their wish will be granted. It's a fun, quirky, and memorable way to spend an evening in Jogja.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located directly south of the Kraton complex. A short walk or becak ride from Prawirotaman.

Opening Hours: Comes alive in the evening, from around 6:00 PM until late.

Cost: Free to enter. Pedal car rental is about IDR 50,000. Blindfolds can be rented for a few thousand rupiah.

Website: N/A

Planning Your Visit to Yogyakarta

Getting Around

Yogyakarta is easy to navigate. Ride-hailing apps like Grab and Gojek are incredibly cheap and convenient for both cars and motorcycle taxis ('ojek'). For a traditional experience, try a 'becak' (cycle rickshaw) for short distances (agree on the price beforehand). The TransJogja bus system is an affordable way to reach major sites like Prambanan. For ultimate freedom, renting a scooter is a popular option, or you can hire a private car with a driver for a full day of sightseeing, which is very affordable.

Where to Stay

Prawirotaman area is a tourist hub, popular with backpackers and mid-range travelers, offering many cafes, tour agencies, and guesthouses. The Malioboro area is perfect if you want to be in the center of the action, close to shopping, food, and the train station. For a more authentic and quiet experience, stay in a guesthouse within the Kraton (palace) walls. Top hotel picks include The Phoenix Hotel Yogyakarta (luxury), Greenhost Boutique Hotel (eco-friendly), and Adhisthana Hotel (stylish mid-range).

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Yogyakarta is during the dry season, from May to September. During these months, you'll have sunny days and minimal rain, which is ideal for temple visits and outdoor activities like volcano treks. The shoulder months of April and October are also pleasant. The wet season runs from November to March, characterized by heavy afternoon downpours, but attractions remain open and the landscape is lush and green.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: Temples & History. Start with a sunrise tour at Borobudur Temple. In the afternoon, explore the majestic Prambanan Temple complex and the nearby Plaosan Temple. End the day with the Ramayana Ballet at Prambanan. Day 2: City Culture. Explore the Kraton (Sultan's Palace) and the adjacent Taman Sari Water Castle. Visit the Sonobudoyo Museum. In the afternoon, learn about batik making or silver crafting in Kota Gede. Spend the evening on Malioboro Street for shopping and street food. Day 3: Nature & Adventure. Choose between an early morning Mount Merapi jeep tour for sunrise views or a caving adventure at Jomblang Cave to see the 'Light of Heaven,' possibly combined with a trip to Timang Beach.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Yogyakarta expensive?
Yogyakarta is one of the most affordable tourist destinations in Southeast Asia. Accommodation is cheap, with guesthouses available for under $15 USD and nice hotels for $30-50 USD. A delicious local meal can cost as little as $2-4 USD. Transportation via ride-hailing apps is very inexpensive. The main costs will be the entrance fees to major sites like Borobudur and Prambanan, and organized tours, which are still very reasonable compared to Western standards.
How many days do you need in Yogyakarta?
A stay of 3 to 4 full days is ideal. This allows you to dedicate one day to the main temples (Borobudur and Prambanan), one day to exploring the city's cultural heart (the Kraton, Taman Sari, Malioboro), and one or two days for adventure activities like Mount Merapi, Jomblang Cave, or exploring the southern beaches. Rushing it in two days is possible but you would miss out on the relaxed pace that makes Jogja so special.
Is Yogyakarta safe?
Yes, Yogyakarta is generally a very safe city for tourists. The locals are known for being incredibly friendly and welcoming. Standard precautions should be taken, as with any city. Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas like Malioboro Street to prevent petty theft. When renting a scooter, always wear a helmet and be aware of the chaotic traffic. Overall, travelers report feeling very safe and comfortable here.
What is Yogyakarta famous for?
Yogyakarta is famous for being the cultural and artistic soul of Java. It's primarily known for its proximity to the world-renowned UNESCO World Heritage sites, Borobudur (Buddhist) and Prambanan (Hindu) temples. It is also famous for traditional Javanese arts like batik textiles, silver craftsmanship (in Kota Gede), gamelan music, and wayang kulit (shadow puppetry). As a royal city still headed by a Sultan, its deep-rooted Javanese court culture is a major draw.
