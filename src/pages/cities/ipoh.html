15 Best Things to Do in Ipoh, Malaysia (2025 Guide)

Nestled in the Kinta Valley and framed by dramatic limestone karst hills, Ipoh is a city of captivating charm and rich history. Once a humble tin-mining town, it has blossomed into a vibrant destination renowned for its colonial-era architecture, stunning cave temples, and a culinary scene that rivals any in Malaysia. The city's slower pace offers a refreshing contrast to the bustle of Kuala Lumpur, inviting visitors to wander through streets adorned with creative murals, sip on the world-famous Ipoh white coffee, and explore ancient caves housing serene places of worship. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or a nature lover, you'll find an incredible array of experiences. This guide will walk you through the absolute best things to do in Ipoh, ensuring a memorable journey into the heart of Perak.

Top 5 Must-Visit Attractions

  • Concubine Lane (Lorong Panglima)
  • Kek Lok Tong Cave Temple and Zen Gardens
  • Ipoh Old Town Murals
  • Tasik Cermin (Mirror Lake)
  • Ipoh Railway Station

Complete Guide to Ipoh Attractions

Concubine Lane (Lorong Panglima)

Once rumored to be a clandestine street where a mining tycoon housed his mistresses, Concubine Lane is now the vibrant heart of Ipoh's Old Town. This narrow alleyway is a bustling hub of activity, packed with souvenir shops, trendy cafes, and street food vendors selling everything from rainbow cheese toast to refreshing ice balls (ais kepal). The lane retains its old-world charm with traditional shophouses and red lanterns strung overhead. It’s the perfect place for a leisurely stroll, people-watching, and capturing colourful photos. While it can get crowded, especially on weekends, the lively atmosphere is part of its appeal. Don't forget to explore the adjacent lanes, Hale Lane and Market Lane, which offer a quieter but equally charming experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in the heart of Ipoh Old Town. It's easily accessible on foot from most parts of the Old Town.

Opening Hours: Shops generally open from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM, with some food stalls opening earlier and closing later.

Cost: Free to walk through. Costs vary for shopping and food.

Website: N/A

Kek Lok Tong Cave Temple and Zen Gardens

Set within a massive cave complex, Kek Lok Tong is one of Ipoh's most impressive cave temples. The main cavern is vast and airy, featuring a central altar with various Buddhist and Taoist deities set against the natural limestone formations. The temple's true gem, however, lies beyond the cave's rear exit. You'll emerge into a beautifully landscaped Zen garden, a hidden valley flanked by towering limestone cliffs. This award-winning garden features a serene lake, a long reflexology path, and manicured lawns, offering a peaceful escape. It's an ideal spot for a quiet walk and to appreciate the harmony between nature and spirituality.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Gunung Rapat, about a 15-minute drive from Ipoh Old Town. Using a ride-hailing service like Grab is recommended.

Opening Hours: 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM daily.

Cost: Free admission, though donations are welcome. Parking is also free.

Website: https://kekloktong.com/

Ipoh Old Town Murals

Lithuanian artist Ernest Zacharevic, famous for his street art in Penang, has also left his mark on Ipoh. His seven murals in the Old Town depict scenes of local life and culture, from an old man sipping coffee to children flying on a paper plane. These interactive and whimsical pieces have breathed new life into the historic streets and created fantastic photo opportunities. Beyond Zacharevic's work, numerous other local artists have contributed to Ipoh's street art scene. Grab a mural map or simply wander the alleys around Jalan Masjid and Jalan Market to discover these creative works. It's a fun, free activity that turns the entire Old Town into an open-air art gallery.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Scattered throughout Ipoh Old Town. A popular starting point is near the OldTown White Coffee original branch.

Opening Hours: Accessible 24/7.

Cost: Free.

Website: N/A - Maps are available at local tourist information centers.

Tasik Cermin (Mirror Lake)

A true hidden gem, Tasik Cermin is a stunning lake concealed behind a limestone hill. To reach it, you must walk through a short, dark tunnel carved through the rock, which adds to the sense of discovery. As you emerge on the other side, you're greeted by a breathtaking sight: a tranquil lake surrounded by lush greenery and towering cliffs. On a calm day, the water's surface perfectly reflects the surrounding scenery, earning it the name 'Mirror Lake'. A jetty allows you to walk out over the water for better views and photos. Recently, boat rides have been introduced to explore the lake further. The seclusion and pristine nature of this spot make it a must-visit for nature lovers and photographers.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located near the Sam Poh Tong temple, about a 15-minute drive from the city center. Best reached by car or Grab.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM daily.

Cost: Around RM 5 per person for entry, with an additional fee for the boat ride.

Website: N/A

Ipoh Railway Station

Affectionately known as the 'Taj Mahal of Ipoh', this magnificent building is a prime example of late-Victorian and Moorish Revival architecture. Designed by Arthur Benison Hubback, the same architect behind the Kuala Lumpur Railway Station, it was completed in 1917. The grand white facade, large central dome, and smaller cupolas make it one of Malaysia's most beautiful railway stations. Still a functioning station, visitors can admire its architecture from the outside or walk through the main hall. The station square features a beautiful garden and the Ipoh Cenotaph, a war memorial. It's a key landmark on the Ipoh Heritage Trail and a testament to the city's wealth during the tin-mining boom.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at Jalan Panglima Bukit Gantang Wahab, at the edge of the Old Town. Easily walkable from other heritage sites.

Opening Hours: Accessible 24/7 as it is an active station.

Cost: Free to visit.

Website: https://www.ktmb.com.my/

Sam Poh Tong Cave Temple

Considered one of the oldest and most famous cave temples in Ipoh, Sam Poh Tong is a significant place of worship with a rich history dating back to the late 19th century. According to legend, the cave was discovered by a Chinese monk who decided to make it his home and place for meditation. The main prayer hall is located within the natural limestone cave, filled with intricate statues of Buddhist deities. A unique feature of this temple is the tortoise pond located within its grounds, where visitors can feed the reptiles, which are symbols of longevity. A climb up a steep flight of 246 steps leads to an open-air viewing deck offering panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Gunung Rapat, close to Kek Lok Tong and a 15-minute drive from the city center.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM daily.

Cost: Free admission, donations are welcome.

Website: N/A

Perak Tong Cave Temple

Perak Tong is another of Ipoh's magnificent cave temples, renowned for its towering 40-foot golden Buddha statue that greets you upon entry. The cave walls are adorned with colourful murals depicting scenes from Chinese mythology and Buddhist scriptures, painted by artists from China. The temple was founded in 1926 by a Buddhist priest from China. For the adventurous, a winding staircase of over 450 steps leads you through the inner recesses of the cave up to an opening at the top of the limestone hill. The climb is rewarded with a spectacular view of Ipoh and its surrounding landscape. The pavilions and pagodas at the top provide a serene spot to rest and take in the scenery.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located along Jalan Kuala Kangsar, about a 15-minute drive north of Ipoh Old Town.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM daily.

Cost: Free admission, donations are appreciated.

Website: N/A

Gua Tempurung

For thrill-seekers and nature enthusiasts, Gua Tempurung is an unmissable adventure. It's one of the largest and longest limestone caves in Peninsular Malaysia, stretching for over 3 kilometers. The cave is a majestic gallery of stalagmites, stalactites, and other incredible rock formations that have been shaped over millions of years. Several tour options are available, ranging from easy, well-lit boardwalk tours on the 'Golden Flowstone' route to more challenging 'Wet Tours' that involve wading through underground rivers, scrambling over rocks, and sliding down natural water slides. It's a fascinating journey into the depths of the earth, guided by experienced professionals.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Gopeng, about a 30-40 minute drive south of Ipoh. A car is necessary to get here.

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM daily. Last tour entry times vary.

Cost: Starts from RM 20 for the dry tour to RM 50 for the full wet tour.

Website: https://guatempurung.com/

Lost World of Tambun

A perfect destination for families, the Lost World of Tambun is a comprehensive theme park set against a backdrop of ancient limestone hills and lush jungle. The park offers a wide variety of attractions, including a water park with thrilling slides, an amusement park with classic rides, and a petting zoo where you can interact with animals like raccoons and giraffes. The park is also known for its natural hot springs, which are open until late at night, providing a relaxing way to end an action-packed day. With additional features like a Tiger Valley, a tin mining history walk, and adventure activities, it offers a full day of entertainment for all ages.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located in Tambun, about a 20-minute drive from Ipoh city center.

Opening Hours: 11:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Mon-Fri), 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Weekends/Holidays). Closed on Tuesdays. Hot springs open until 11:00 PM.

Cost: Ticket prices vary, check the official website for current rates and promotions. Around RM 117 for adults.

Website: https://sunwaylostworldoftambun.com/

Birch Memorial Clock Tower

Standing proudly in front of the State Mosque, the Birch Memorial Clock Tower is a significant historical landmark in Ipoh. It was erected in 1909 in memory of J.W.W. Birch, the first British Resident of Perak, who was assassinated in 1875. The clock tower's design is intriguing, featuring four panels that depict the 'Growth of Civilization' by four different figures: Moses, Buddha, Shakespeare, and Charles Darwin. The main bell is flanked by four smaller bells which strike the chimes. The tower itself is a beautiful structure that serves as a focal point for the historic square, which also includes the Ipoh Town Hall and the Old Post Office. It's an essential stop on any heritage walk through the city.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located at Jalan Dato Sagor in Ipoh Old Town, near the Ipoh Railway Station.

Opening Hours: Accessible 24/7.

Cost: Free.

Website: N/A

Ho Yan Hor Museum

Discover the fascinating story behind one of Malaysia's most famous household brands at the Ho Yan Hor Museum. This small but well-curated museum tells the tale of Dr. Ho Kai Cheong, the creator of the renowned Ho Yan Hor herbal tea. The museum is housed in the very building where Dr. Ho first lived and ran his business. Through personal artifacts, photographs, and interactive displays, you'll learn about the tea's origins as a remedy for the common cold and its journey to becoming a national icon. The visit ends with a complimentary cup of the famous herbal tea. It offers a unique and personal glimpse into Ipoh's entrepreneurial history.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Jalan Bijeh Timah in Ipoh Old Town, a short walk from Concubine Lane.

Opening Hours: 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM (Tuesday - Sunday). Closed on Mondays.

Cost: Free admission.

Website: https://www.horyanhor.com/en/museum/

Qing Xin Ling Leisure & Cultural Village

Step back in time at Qing Xin Ling Leisure & Cultural Village, a nostalgic attraction nestled amidst limestone hills and a serene lake. The name translates to 'Serene Hill's Spirit', and it offers just that. The village is filled with vintage and replica items from the 1960s and 70s, from old street stalls and classic cars to antique furniture and memorabilia. Visitors can rent bicycles or trishaws to explore the grounds, feed fish in the lake, or simply relax in one of the many traditional huts. The entire village is designed to be highly photogenic and interactive, making it a fun and lighthearted experience for visitors of all ages looking for a dose of nostalgia and beautiful scenery.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located near Gunung Rapat, a 20-minute drive from the city center. Best reached by car or Grab.

Opening Hours: 9:30 AM to 5:00 PM daily. Closed on Sundays.

Cost: Around RM 12 per person for entry.

Website: https://www.facebook.com/qingxinling/

Indulge in Ipoh's Culinary Scene

A trip to Ipoh is incomplete without a dedicated culinary tour. The city is a food lover's paradise, famous for several iconic dishes. Start your day with a cup of authentic Ipoh White Coffee, a unique brew where coffee beans are roasted with palm oil margarine, giving it a distinct, smooth flavor. Pair it with kaya toast and soft-boiled eggs. For lunch or dinner, seek out Nga Choy Kai (Bean Sprout Chicken), a simple yet delicious dish of poached chicken served with blanched bean sprouts that are uniquely plump and crunchy thanks to Ipoh's hard water from the surrounding hills. Other must-tries include silky Caramel Custard, fluffy Hakka Mee, rich Kai See Hor Fun (shredded chicken noodle soup), and flaky egg tarts from local bakeries. Exploring Ipoh's food is an attraction in itself.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Famous food stalls and restaurants are concentrated in Old Town and New Town. Key spots include Nam Heong White Coffee, Restoran Lou Wong Tauge Ayam Kue Tiau, and Funny Mountain Soya Beancurd.

Opening Hours: Varies by establishment. Many are open from early morning until late at night.

Cost: Very affordable. A full meal can cost between RM 10-20.

Website: N/A

Funtasy House Trick Art

Inject some playful fun into your Ipoh trip with a visit to Funtasy House Trick Art. This interactive museum features over 20 hand-painted 3D murals and optical illusions that allow you to become part of the art. Pose as if you're surfing a big wave, crossing a rickety bridge, or being chased by a dinosaur. It's a fantastic indoor activity, especially on a hot or rainy day, and is particularly popular with families and groups of friends. The staff are friendly and happy to help you get the best angles for your photos. Be sure to bring a fully charged camera or phone for an amusing and memorable experience.

Practical Information

How to Get There: Located on Jalan Market in Ipoh Old Town, making it easy to combine with a heritage walk.

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Weekdays), 9:00 AM to 8:00 PM (Weekends/Holidays).

Cost: Around RM 19 for adults and RM 10 for children/seniors.

Website: https://www.facebook.com/FuntasyHouseTrickArt/

Ipoh Heritage Trail

The best way to appreciate Ipoh's colonial past is by taking a self-guided walk along the Ipoh Heritage Trail. This trail covers the key architectural gems of the Old Town, most of which were built during the tin-mining boom in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The route is well-marked and easy to follow with a map from the tourist information center. Key buildings along the trail include the magnificent Ipoh Railway Station, the stately Town Hall and Old Post Office, the Birch Memorial Clock Tower, the High Court, and numerous old shophouses that showcase a blend of British colonial and Chinese architectural styles. The walk provides a deep appreciation for the city's history and the wealth that once flowed through its streets.

Practical Information

How to Get There: The trail is centered around the Ipoh Old Town area. A good starting point is the Ipoh Railway Station.

Opening Hours: The trail is outdoors and accessible 24/7.

Cost: Free.

Website: N/A - Trail maps are available at the Ipoh Tourist Information Centre.

Planning Your Visit to Ipoh

Getting Around

Ipoh's Old Town is very walkable. For attractions further afield, such as the cave temples or Gua Tempurung, the ride-hailing service Grab is widely available and affordable. Renting a car is also a good option for those wanting maximum flexibility to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Stay

For historic charm and walkability, stay in Ipoh Old Town. For modern amenities and shopping, Greentown is a great choice. Recommendations: The Banjaran Hotsprings Retreat (Luxury), M Roof Hotel & Residences (Mid-Range), and Container Hotel Ipoh (Budget).

Best Time to Visit

Ipoh has a tropical climate and is warm year-round. The driest months are typically between March and September, which are ideal for exploring. The city can get very busy during Malaysian public holidays and school holidays, so book accommodation in advance or visit during off-peak periods for a quieter experience.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: Explore Ipoh Old Town. Walk the Heritage Trail, discover the street art murals, visit Concubine Lane, and indulge in white coffee and local snacks. Day 2: Go on a cave temple tour. Visit Kek Lok Tong, Sam Poh Tong, and Perak Tong. In the afternoon, discover the hidden Tasik Cermin. Day 3: Choose your adventure. Either spend the day at the Lost World of Tambun theme park or go spelunking at Gua Tempurung. End the day with a final feast of Ipoh's famous cuisine.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ipoh expensive?
Ipoh is considered one of the most affordable travel destinations in Malaysia. Food, in particular, is very cheap, with delicious meals available for just a few dollars. Accommodation and transportation are also reasonably priced, making it a great value-for-money destination.
How many days do you need in Ipoh?
A stay of 2 to 3 days is ideal to cover the main highlights of Ipoh. This gives you enough time to explore the Old Town, visit a few cave temples, and enjoy the food scene without feeling rushed. If you plan to visit attractions like Gua Tempurung or Lost World of Tambun, consider adding an extra day.
Is Ipoh safe?
Yes, Ipoh is generally a very safe city for tourists. The locals are friendly and welcoming. Standard travel precautions should be taken, such as being aware of your surroundings in crowded places and keeping your valuables secure. Violent crime is rare.
What is Ipoh famous for?
Ipoh is most famous for three things: its incredible local cuisine, especially Ipoh White Coffee and Bean Sprout Chicken; its stunning limestone cave temples that are set within dramatic karst hills; and its well-preserved British colonial-era architecture in the Old Town.
